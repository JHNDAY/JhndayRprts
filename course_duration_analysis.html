Calculation Methodology

10-Week Equivalent Formula:

10-Week Equivalents = ⌊5-Week Courses ÷ 2⌋ + 10-Week Courses

(⌊ ⌋ represents floor division - ignoring remainders)

Step-by-Step Calculations:

Online Courses:

  • 5-week courses: 19 courses
  • 10-week courses: 20 courses
  • 10-week equivalents from 5-week: 19 ÷ 2 = 9 equivalents (1 course remainder)
  • Total 10-week equivalents: 9 + 20 = 29

In-Person Courses:

  • 5-week courses: 13 courses
  • 10-week courses: 6 courses
  • 10-week equivalents from 5-week: 13 ÷ 2 = 6 equivalents (1 course remainder)
  • Total 10-week equivalents: 6 + 6 = 12

Key Findings

⚠️ Significant Choice Disparity

Your observation is confirmed: A student who wants only in-person courses has significantly fewer options available.

  • Online students have access to 29 ten-week equivalent course options
  • In-person students have access to only 12 ten-week equivalent course options
  • This represents a 2.4:1 ratio - online students have more than twice as many equivalent course options
  • The disparity is even more pronounced than the raw course count (39 vs 19) because in-person courses have proportionally fewer 10-week offerings

Course Duration Patterns

  • Online courses have a balanced mix: 19 five-week + 20 ten-week
  • In-person courses are predominantly five-week: 13 five-week + 6 ten-week
  • Total offerings: 32 five-week courses and 26 ten-week courses across both delivery methods

Methodology Notes

  • Duration identified by course codes: "-5a-" and "-5b-" for 5-week courses, "-10-" for 10-week courses
  • All 58 courses were successfully categorized using these patterns
  • Remainder courses (1 online 5-week, 1 in-person 5-week) don't contribute to 10-week equivalents in this calculation
  • Based on the principle that students are entitled to "the equivalent of two 10-week courses"
