Thematic analysis of 17 BOLLI registrant course expectations, grouped into four clusters: build a foundation (5), practical application (5), overcome barriers (3), basic familiarity (3), plus one off-topic response.

Four expectation clusters — 16 students

Build a foundation 5

Self-described novices; want to go from zero to functional. Some cite computer literacy gaps preceding AI.

"I am definitely an A.I. illiterate. I hope the course will take me through the basics."
"First, improve my basic keyboard and desktop skills as necessary preconditions to using AI tools."
Practical application 5

Purpose-driven learners who have a clear use case — research, teaching, art history, coursework — and want AI to serve it.

"Learn to effectively use Claude to research art history... saving work in intermediate or finished form for future reference."
"Enhance my research capacity and learn alternative methods of presenting results to my classes."
Overcome barriers 3

Express a specific emotional or epistemic obstacle — fear, confusion, or distrust of AI outputs — more than a skill gap.

"Get over fear of AI and the feeling of not knowing for sure if an answer is factual."
"Decrease my confusion. With luck and effort raise myself to a respectable lower intermediate level."
Basic familiarity 3

Have touched ChatGPT or similar in a limited way; want a broader, more confident working relationship with AI tools.

"Become more familiar with AI capabilities and limitations."
"I use ChatGPT occasionally but in a limited one-question manner."

Instructor flags — responses warranting individual attention

Wants to repeat the course

"I do not think one introduction is enough for me!" — plan for reinforcement loops or office-hours follow-on.

Pre-AI computer anxiety

One student lists basic keyboard/desktop skills as a prerequisite — may need bridging support before the AI content lands.

Factual accuracy concern

Explicitly names hallucination/trust as the core problem — a teaching moment to build in early, not assume.

SGL with advanced goals

One SGL wants to use AI in their own courses and present research differently — an outlier who may need enrichment.
