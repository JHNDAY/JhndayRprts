What each tier needs from the course

Computer-first — 5 students

29% of cohort

Need bridging support that addresses the computer layer before the AI layer. AI instruction delivered on top of unresolved computer anxiety will not stick.

"First, improve my basic keyboard and desktop skills as necessary preconditions."
"Don't know how to bookmark, or split screen, or transfer information from zoom chat."

Core AI learners — 8 students

47% of cohort

The course should be designed for this group. Digitally functional but with no mental model of how AI works, what it's for, or whether to trust it.

"Familiarity with AI — I use ChatGPT occasionally but in a limited one-question manner."
"Decrease my confusion. Raise myself to a respectable lower intermediate level."

Purpose-driven / advanced — 4 students

24% of cohort

Digitally confident. Need AI-specific depth: multi-turn prompting, output workflows, visual tools, research pipelines. Will disengage if kept at orientation level.

"I'd like to become more skilled at using them efficiently and integrate visual capabilities."
"Learn alternative methods of presenting the results of my research to my classes."
