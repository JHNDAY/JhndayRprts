BOLLI Focus Group Analysis

Strategic Planning Initiative - Member Experience & Vision

Focus Group Composition

Participants: 6 long-term BOLLI members (pilot group for strategic planning)

Experience Range: 2-10 years at BOLLI

Backgrounds: Diverse professional experience including education, law, medicine, and technology

Location: Greater Boston area residents

Session Overview

This was a pilot focus group conducted as part of BOLLI's strategic planning process. Participants discussed their experiences, challenges, and vision for the future of lifelong learning at BOLLI.

Experience at BOLLI

Standout Learning Experiences
Taking Proust... 7 semesters of absolutely classic book that we always wanted to read. We had incredible Professor... it was really one of my most amazing learning experiences.

Top-rated experiences:

Proust Literature Course New Yorker Discussion Group Book Groups Hands-on Workshops
Volunteer Leadership Impact
Being on the curriculum committee... you feel like you're actually part of the creation of it. And it's very different from just coming in for classes and leaving.

Leadership roles valued:

Curriculum Committee Teaching/Facilitating Study Group Leadership Course Development
Major Challenges
Physical Facility: Basement location with poor ventilation, sound issues, and accessibility concerns
Technology Support: Outdated systems and inadequate technical assistance
Limited Committee Opportunities: Few volunteer positions available for interested members
Welcoming New Members: Difficulty making personal connections, especially on Zoom

Learning Preferences & Needs

Preferred Learning Formats
I don't have the attention span for a purely lecture course... there has to be some interactivity. I like us to talk to each other in class.

Format preferences:

Interactive Discussion Peer-to-Peer Learning Reading + Questions Mixed Media (videos, slides)
Zoom vs. In-Person Debate
We all know it's harder to get to know people [on Zoom]. You don't have that wandering out to the parking lot together, chatting after a class kind of opportunity.

Split preferences:

  • Pro-Zoom: Convenience, accessibility, weather independence
  • Pro-In-Person: Better connections, community building, natural interactions
  • Consensus: Need both options available
Course Quality Factors
The quality of the course is really virtually entirely dependent on the SGL [Study Group Leader].

Key quality indicators:

Engaged Participants Skilled Facilitator Interactive Format Appropriate Materials

Learning Community

Community as Core Value
For 10 years now... I have had the opportunity multiple times a week, all year round, to talk, to get to know, and become friendly with just a really wonderful group of people.

Community elements valued:

Year-round Engagement Intellectual Stimulation Social Connections Shared Interests
Welcoming Challenges
Nobody talked to me. I didn't have a placard that had my name... nobody said Welcome! Nobody said, What's your name?

Integration barriers identified:

  • Lack of structured welcome process
  • Existing members not reaching out
  • Limited opportunities for casual interaction
  • Zoom making connections even harder
Physical Space Impact
I appreciate the fact that there is a room in the middle where everyone has to go in order to go to their class... that common room is important.

Importance of central gathering space:

Crossroads Design Casual Encounters Community Building Shared Lunch Space

Future Vision for BOLLI

Balancing Academic Rigor & Accessibility
I would like to keep the intellectual rigor of the courses. I don't think that we hurt those by adding some hands-on or different kinds of courses.
Maintain: Small liberal arts college atmosphere with intellectual depth
Add: More practical "how-to" courses and hands-on learning
Expand: Variety in course formats and teaching styles
Addressing Generational Needs
I kind of feel that all of us are in an elder category of BOLLI members, and there are newer people coming in who are as much as 20 and 30 years younger than we are.

Generational considerations:

  • Younger members (50s-60s) may want different course types
  • Need input from newer/younger demographic
  • Balance traditional academic focus with evolving preferences
Operational Improvements
Facility Upgrade: Better physical space with improved ventilation and accessibility
Technology Enhancement: Modern systems with reliable technical support
Course Marketing: Better promotion of underenrolled classes through profiles and previews
Member Integration: Structured welcome process and mentorship programs
Volunteer Expansion: More committee opportunities for member engagement
Course Development Strategy
We need to do some of these things. First of all, get more members being SGLs, and that's more affordable. So there's a double plus for that.

Strategic recommendations:

More Member Instructors Balanced Course Portfolio Better Course Matching Instructor Support
