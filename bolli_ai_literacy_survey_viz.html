Registrant Profile Dashboard

Profiles the 17 enrolled students across topic interests (Current Events leads at 15 of 17), device type (Mac 12, PC 5), browser (Safari dominant at 7), and attendance preference (8 prefer hybrid).

Grounds the entire curriculum in who is actually in the room — the Current Events and Literature majority validates the course's applied, real-world framing rather than a purely technical one.

Course Expectations Analysis

Clusters the 17 students' stated expectations into four groups: Build a Foundation (5), Practical Application (5), Overcome Barriers (3), and Basic Familiarity (3). Flags four students warranting individual attention — including one who wants to repeat the course.

Reveals that equal weight must be given to fear-reduction as to skill-building; the course cannot open with capability demonstrations before first addressing the trust and anxiety issues three students named explicitly.

Curriculum Arc

Maps the full session-by-session arc of the course — from first-contact fundamentals through applied use cases — as a structured visual progression rather than a flat session list.

Makes the pedagogical logic visible to students, reinforcing the "learning by doing with a purpose" philosophy and letting participants orient themselves within the arc at any moment in the course.

4
tech environment

Device & Browser Combinations

Renders a Mac × Browser matrix across all 17 students. Mac + Safari accounts for 7 students — the largest single cohort — while Mac + Chrome (3) and PC + Chrome (3) follow. Only 1 student uses Edge.

The Safari-majority finding is operationally critical: any HTML artifact or interactive tool used in-session must be Safari-tested first. The known Safari regex-flag limitation directly affects the token prediction artifact for nearly half the class.

student positioning

Digital Fluency vs. AI Readiness

Plots students on two independent axes — general digital fluency and AI-specific readiness — producing a four-quadrant map that separates digitally confident AI novices from those who face both hurdles simultaneously.

The most actionable pre-course viz: students in the low-fluency / low-readiness quadrant need a fundamentally different entry point than the "practical application" cluster. Informs peer-pairing, pacing decisions, and whether a bridging session is warranted before session one.
