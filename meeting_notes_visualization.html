📊 Detailed Summary

📅 Class Scheduling and Space Solutions

Ted discussed space allocation and scheduling challenges for classes, emphasizing the need to address in-person and Zoom conflicts. He proposed a three-term solution with 8-week classes, but noted that implementing this immediately might not be feasible. Instead, he suggested prioritizing easier solutions like evening classes or expanding to twice or once-a-week sessions, which could be implemented sooner. Ted also raised questions about the capacity of the new space and whether more in-person classes could be accommodated without changes.

💳 Pay-Per-Course Model Implementation Challenges

Ted discussed the risks and complexities of implementing a pay-per-course model, expressing concern about its financial implications and operational challenges. He suggested exploring options to open specific short-term sessions to the public without affecting existing membership structures. Ted also noted that while some members object to the current tuition, it was not a significant barrier to membership, and conversion issues were more related to connection and timing.

👥 Trial Membership Engagement Strategies

Ted discussed the efforts of the membership committee to welcome and engage trial members, who have limited access to classes but can participate in other activities. He emphasized the importance of personal contact through liaisons to help trial members navigate the organization and track metrics like conversion to full membership and retention rates. Ted highlighted the complexity of the organization as a strength, preventing member boredom, while acknowledging areas for improvement, such as simplifying the website and catalog. He also mentioned a new member's positive experience at a welcome event, noting that even existing members may not be aware of certain benefits, like bringing a friend to events.

📚 Initiatives and Curriculum Planning

Ted and the team discussed several initiatives, including archiving recordings in a library for a fee, expanding social media presence, and exploring partnerships for curriculum planning. They decided to create a Facebook group with restricted access and considered hiring a social media intern to manage it. The group also discussed the need for intentional curriculum planning, noting they were already working on this with the BSO. They agreed to remove a survey about member desires and focus on implementing these initiatives with available resources.

🎭 Theater Program and Community Collaboration

Ted discussed a 10-week program involving theater classes and performances, similar to programs at Harvard and UMass Boston. He proposed collaborating with local theaters like the Huntington and Lyric to offer discounted matinee performances followed by talkbacks, with classes meeting both on-site and at theaters. The group also touched on member surveys, marketing efforts, and fundraising opportunities, while noting that some items, like scheduling changes and lottery issues, were already addressed elsewhere.

🎲 Lottery System and Funding Challenges

Ted expressed confusion about how people consistently get their first choice in the lottery system without understanding the rules, sharing an example of a long-time member who failed to get any courses due to not signing up for six classes. He emphasized the importance of ensuring participants sign up for the required number of classes to receive their choices and discussed the need to address funding issues by either increasing volunteer rates or finding additional funding. Ted also suggested creating a structured plan to categorize actions into short-term, medium-term, and long-term goals for future meetings.

💻 Enhancing Zoom Class Support Strategies

The group discussed concerns about Zoom classes, with Ted emphasizing the need to support the Zoom community, which comprises 60% of members, while not ignoring in-person classes. They identified issues with Zoom technical support, member engagement, and platform access, and discussed potential solutions including adjusting class times to 5 PM instead of 7 PM to improve attendance. The team agreed to work on specific ideas for supporting Zoom members before the next meeting, and discussed marketing strategies to reach Brandeis alumni, with Ted sharing positive experiences from a hybrid class at Hebrew College that successfully integrated Zoom and in-person learning.
