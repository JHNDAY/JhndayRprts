Marjorie Taylor Greene's Gaza Statements

First Republican to Call Israel's Actions "Genocide"

Historic Break from GOP

Greene becomes the first Republican member of Congress to describe Israel's actions in Gaza as genocide, breaking significantly from her party's traditionally strong support for Israel.

Sources: The Hill (1-1), Haaretz (5-1)
Primary Genocide Accusation
"It's the most truthful and easiest thing to say that Oct 7th in Israel was horrific and all hostages must be returned, but so is the genocide, humanitarian crisis, and starvation happening in Gaza."
Social Media Post - Al Jazeera, USA Today
TV Elaboration
"Israel is not hurting, and they've already proven that they are more than capable of not only defending themselves, but annihilating their enemies to the point of genocide. And that's what's happening in Gaza."
Source: The Hill (1-1) - One America News Network
Systematic Cleansing Claims
Netanyahu's government is "capable of and in the process of systematically cleansing them from the land" and "we no longer have to fund and fight nuclear armed secular Israel's wars, especially when it leads to starving children and killing innocent people, including Christians."
Source: Haaretz (5-1)
Moral Equivalency Question
"Are innocent Israeli lives more valuable than innocent Palestinian and Christian lives? And why should America continue funding this?"
Source: The Hill (6-1)
Call to End U.S. Support
Called on her GOP colleagues "to stop funding foreign wars," specifically referencing the Israel-Hamas conflict in Gaza.
Source: Haaretz (5-1)
Response to Criticism
Pushing back against allegations that criticism of Israel is "antisemitic," Greene wrote that she "refuse[s] that manipulative label."
Source: Haaretz (5-1)

Political Impact & Reactions

AIPAC Response

Called Greene's comments "disgusting" and accused her of betraying "American values," likening her to progressive Democrats

Party Break

First Republican to use "genocide" terminology, creating rare GOP split on Israel policy

Trump Alliance

Puts strain on relationship with Trump, who remains strongly pro-Israel

2026 Elections

AIPAC has not announced plans to challenge Greene, who won by 30 points in 2024
