🎥 Multi-Stream Technology Explained

▼

Multi-stream capability allows individual students to appear as separate Zoom participants rather than a single combined video feed.

Critical Finding: Only 4 systems provide genuine multi-stream capability for individual student framing as separate Zoom participants.

🏗️ Room Configuration Requirements

▼

Camera Placement

  • Mount at front-center of 25-foot width
  • Height: 7-8 feet above floor
  • Position as close as possible to display showing remote students
  • Critical: Systems must cover students 15-25 feet away (second row)

Dual Display Strategy

  • Display 1: 75-85" showing remote participant gallery view
  • Display 2: Presentation content and shared materials
  • 4K resolution essential for 14+ participant views
  • Mount behind presenter to create unified presence

Acoustic Treatment

  • Target: 0.6 seconds or less RT60 for speech clarity
  • Acoustically-rated ceiling tiles (NRC 0.70-0.95)
  • Fabric acoustic panels on 20-30% of wall surface
  • Carpet or rubber chair caps to prevent echo

Lighting Specifications

  • 300-500 lux at desk level
  • LED fixtures: 3000-3500K color temperature
  • CRI 90+ for accurate skin tones
  • Blackout capability for windows behind students

🌐 Network & Technical Requirements

▼

Bandwidth Requirements

  • Upload: 30-50 Mbps dedicated (not shared)
  • Download: 10-15 Mbps for 14 remote feeds
  • Multi-camera systems add 10-15 Mbps per camera
  • QoS network configuration prioritizing video traffic

Network Configuration

  • Dedicated VLAN for classroom AV equipment
  • DSCP tagging (Audio: DSCP 46, Video: DSCP 34)
  • Prioritize UDP ports 8801-8810
  • Hardwired Ethernet connections (no WiFi)
  • UPS for clean shutdown protection

📚 Pedagogical Best Practices

▼

Equitable Participation Strategies

  • Alternate calling patterns between in-person and remote students
  • Use participation tracking checklists
  • Wait 7-10 seconds after questions for chat responses
  • Assign a "chat monitor" role for remote contributions
  • Use polling tools (Poll Everywhere, Mentimeter)
  • Enable Zoom reactions for quick feedback

Breakout Room Best Practice

Recommended Approach: Have ALL students (including in-person) join Zoom from individual devices with headphones. This enables true mixed breakout rooms with both in-person and remote students as equal participants.

Recording & Compliance

  • Use Zoom cloud recording (automatic upload)
  • Record in "Gallery View with Shared Screen" layout
  • Enable FERPA compliance settings
  • Limit access to enrolled students only
  • Set sharing to "authenticated users only"
  • 2-year retention for review

Important Reality Check

Current technology cannot automatically frame all 14 in-person students as 14 separate Zoom participants. The best systems show 4-5 individually framed participants (active speakers) plus room overview.

For truly individual representation of every student, have in-person students join Zoom from personal devices with headphones.

Pre-Launch Testing Checklist

  • Full dress rehearsal with actual students
  • Test from both in-person and remote perspectives
  • Verify all 14 students visible, especially back row
  • Confirm audio pickup for soft-spoken students
  • Practice breakout rooms and content sharing
  • Test whiteboard capture
  • Adjust camera positioning and lighting
  • Optimize Zoom settings based on results
