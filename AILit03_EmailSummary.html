Session Highlights

Spring 2026 · In-room & Remote · Instructor: John Day

  • ◈ The meaning of words — Three levels of semantic depth: basic definition, contextual usage, and word embeddings (a word's presence across millions of texts). Understanding these is the key to writing effective prompts and to grasping why AI can search with meaning, not just with keywords.
  • ◈ Generative AI vs. RAG, revisited — Building on Session 2, the class dug deeper into how retrieval-augmented generation works. The analogy: writing a high school research paper — gathering sources, weighing them, incorporating what fits — now running at incomprehensible speed. Human revision remains non-negotiable."The machine finds; the human verifies, contextualizes, decides what matters." — Murrow
  • 🌐 Convivencia — The session's most resonant conceptual moment: John drew a parallel between medieval Iberian cooperation (Jews, Muslims, Christians producing scholarship neither could alone) and the human–AI partnership. Not assimilation, not dominance — genuine collaboration across difference."The moment either partner assumes dominance, the collaboration collapses." — Murrow
  • 🖥 Visualization demo — Live demonstration of how a plain-text AI response transforms into a designed HTML artifact: color, position, typography, icons. Participants grasped quickly that the same trillion web pages that trained the model also teach it good design. "That's really impressive that you do that."
  • 🎙 Ed Murrow avatar — trigger protocol in practice — The avatar's voice/text trigger system was demonstrated live throughout the session across multiple exchanges. Context-window congestion surfaced mid-session; John reset to a fresh conversation — itself a teaching moment about how token accumulation degrades AI response quality.
  • 🔗 Levels of collaboration — Three tiers identified: (1) individual using AI for personal research, (2) a group sharing ideas with AI as moderator/facilitator, (3) BOLLI's diverse life and professional experience amplified through AI partnership. The class is itself a live example of tier two.
  • ◈ "The AI SIG" — a proposal — John outlined a new monthly practicum group to extend the course beyond five sessions: less lecture, more doing and sharing. Participants would design a course of their own — whether they ever teach it or not — as the core project. Final class will shape the plan."It forces them to think like educators, which means thinking rigorously about what matters." — Murrow
  • ⚠ Key friction point — Voice-trigger channel congestion caused Murrow's responses to become halting; a mid-session restart resolved it.
