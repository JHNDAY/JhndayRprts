Understanding When AI Thinks With You vs. Researches For You

The Good News: You don't need perfect prompts! AI excels at taking your "fruit salad" of ideas‚Äîeven stream-of-consciousness thoughts‚Äîand producing organized, useful output. But understanding AI's two modes helps you get even better results.

What It Does:

The AI organizes, analyzes, and develops ideas using knowledge from its training.

Think of it like:

A knowledgeable colleague who remembers and organizes ideas from their expertise

Best For:

  • Brainstorming course structures
  • Refining learning objectives
  • Developing thematic connections
  • Organizing messy ideas into coherent plans
  • Exploring concepts and relationships

What It Does:

The AI searches external sources (the web), then reads and synthesizes what it finds.

Think of it like:

That same colleague going to the library, finding relevant sources, and explaining what they found

Best For:

  • Current events and recent news
  • Specific facts and statistics
  • Finding authoritative sources
  • Verifying up-to-date information
  • Discovering new resources

Use "Thinking With Me" When:

  • You need help organizing your thoughts
  • You're developing course structure or learning objectives
  • You want to explore connections between ideas
  • You're brainstorming or refining concepts
  • The topic is established knowledge (not breaking news)

Use "Research For Me" When:

  • You need current events or recent developments
  • You want specific facts, dates, or statistics
  • You're looking for authoritative sources to cite
  • You need to verify or fact-check information
  • You want to discover new resources or materials
Pro Tip: Often you'll use both! Start with Mode 1 to organize your thinking, then switch to Mode 2 to fill in current facts and find resources.
