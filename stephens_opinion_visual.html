THE NEW YORK TIMES
OPINION

No, Israel Is Not Committing Genocide in Gaza

By Bret Stephens, Opinion Columnist
July 22, 2025

Central Argument

If Israel truly intended genocide, why hasn't the death toll been vastly higher given Israel's military capabilities?

The Core Question

"Why isn't the death count higher?"

~60,000
Total deaths cited by Hamas-run Health Ministry (combatant and civilian combined) over nearly 2 years
Military Capacity Argument
Israel has demonstrated restraint despite having the military capacity for far greater destruction. It routinely warns civilians to evacuate, puts its own soldiers at risk in ground operations, and could have bombed without notice but chose not to.
Legal Definition
Genocide requires "intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a national, ethnical, racial or religious group, as such." War casualties alone do not constitute genocide - the key elements are intent and targeting people simply for their group membership.
Historical Precedent
The U.S.-backed operation in Mosul (2016-2017) against ISIS resulted in up to 11,000 civilian deaths over 9 months with broad international support and no genocide accusations. Similar urban warfare tactics were employed.
Strategic Constraints
Israel isn't deterred by hostages (it knows their locations), diplomatic pressure (Trump supports tougher action), or economic threats (Tel Aviv stock exchange has been world's best-performing since Oct. 7).

UN Definition of Genocide

"Acts committed with intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a national, ethnical, racial or religious group, as such."

Key Elements: Intent + targeting people for group membership alone

Gaza vs. Historical Examples

True Genocide (Holocaust)

Nazis systematically murdered Jews specifically because they were Jews, with deliberate intent to exterminate the group.

True Genocide (Rwanda)

Hutus systematically slaughtered Tutsis specifically because they were Tutsi, with intent to destroy the group.

War Casualties (WWII)

Over 1 million German civilians died, but the goal was defeating Nazis for their actions, not exterminating Germans as Germans.

Gaza Conflict

Israel's stated aims: return hostages and eliminate Hamas as military threat. Not targeting Palestinians as Palestinians.

Why the "Genocide" Label Matters

Antisemitic Weaponization

Used by anti-Zionists to equate Israel with Nazi Germany, licensing new wave of Jew hatred

Term Dilution

Overuse risks blinding us to actual genocides when they occur by cheapening the term's meaning

Historical Pattern

Continues pattern of inflated charges against Israel that don't withstand close scrutiny

Violence Incitement

False equivalency with Nazi Germany has led to real-world violence against Jewish targets

Hamas War Crimes

Unlike Ukraine where civilians go underground while military fights above, Hamas reverses this: hiding in tunnels while civilians remain exposed

Stephens' Conclusion

The war in Gaza should end, but calling it genocide does nothing to advance that goal. Instead, it dilutes the meaning of a word we cannot afford to cheapen and risks blinding us to real genocides when they occur.
