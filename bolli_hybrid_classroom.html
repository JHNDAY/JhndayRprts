Access Expert SGLs Globally
Renowned Harvard professor in London can co-teach with local Brandeis faculty. Remote experts share specialized knowledge without travel constraints, expanding BOLLI's academic reach.
2
Health & Mobility Support
SGMs with health challenges, mobility issues, or recovery periods stay connected to their learning community. No one misses out due to physical limitations or seasonal health concerns.
3
Eliminate Commute Barriers
Boston-area traffic and parking concerns dissolve. Cape Cod and suburban members join easily. Weather becomes irrelevant. Geographic boundaries expand BOLLI's reach.
4
Preserve In-Person Connection
Coffee conversations, spontaneous discussions, and the energy of shared physical space remain intact. Body language, side conversations, and post-class socializing flourish.
5
Virtual Community Building
Pre-class Zoom social time, chat conversations during sessions, and optional post-class "parking lot" discussions create meaningful connections among remote participants.
6
Enhanced Body Language Sharing
Strategic camera placement captures facial expressions and gestures. Remote participants see presenter enthusiasm and peer reactions. Multiple camera angles ensure no one feels excluded.
7
SGM Contribution Optimization
Discussion-based courses thrive with diverse perspectives. Chat allows thoughtful written contributions. Breakout rooms enable small group dynamics mixing in-person and virtual participants.
8
Collaborative Co-Learning
SGMs become co-teachers sharing expertise. Virtual whiteboards enable collaborative note-taking. Digital resources are instantly accessible to all participants regardless of location.
9
Optimized Learning Experience
Best of both worlds: intimate discussion groups, diverse perspectives, expert access, and flexible participation. Recordings help with complex topics. Technology enhances rather than replaces human connection.

The BOLLI Advantage

This hybrid model honors BOLLI's core values: intellectual engagement, community connection, and lifelong learning. It removes barriers while preserving the spontaneous discussions and peer relationships that make BOLLI special. Whether you're a retired physician in Lexington preferring in-person interaction or a former tech executive on the Cape avoiding winter drives, you're equally part of the BOLLI learning community.
