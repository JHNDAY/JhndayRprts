STEP 1: Barbara's Simple Narrative

Barbara submitted an unstructured text describing her course on aging and generational differences‚Äîcovering demographics, ageism, and "generational blame." No formatting, just natural language expressing her vision.

STEP 2: Split-Screen Setup

John demonstrated split-screen workflow: Google Doc on left, Claude on right. This setup (works on Chrome, Mac or PC) allows you to adjust screen proportions and move content seamlessly between platforms.

STEP 3: Copy Barbara's Narrative

STEP 4: Paste + "Context" Command

The Critical Technique:
"This is context, don't respond."

John pasted Barbara's narrative, then added this crucial instruction before hitting Enter. This tells Claude to absorb the information without generating a response yet.

Lois's Helpful Tip
"If you're in Claude, if you hit shift and enter, it doesn't trigger it."

This back-and-forth showed participants that prompts are flexible and should be tailored to their specific teaching context.

STEP 5.5: The "Memory" Advantage

John added: "This course is given at BOLLI." He explained that Claude remembers his hundreds of hours of conversation about developing BOLLI courses, so this single phrase automatically incorporates that accumulated context into the course design.

Deep Research Workflow
