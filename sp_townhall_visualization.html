Four Strategic Goals

1 Enhancing the Curriculum
  • Issue RFPs for desired courses to fill gaps
  • Increase member SGLs: Recruitment, training, mentorship, co-teaching
  • Flexible scheduling: Mini-courses, varied session lengths, evening classes, summer/winter sessions
  • Enlarge SGL toolbox: Beyond Zoom - trained co-hosts, digital literacy support, AI tools instruction
  • Add non-traditional courses: Research other programs, partner with cultural institutions
  • Expand partnerships: Other OSHER programs, Dartmouth lecture series on AI
2 Strengthening Community
  • Emphasize community in all messaging and materials
  • Expand orientation/mentorship at all program stages
  • Informal interactions: Before/after classes, lunch gatherings, coffee hours, name tags
  • Classroom experiences: Field trips, breakout sessions, social components
  • Pre-session meet-and-greets to separate social from academic content
  • Enhance Distinguished Speaker Series: Hybrid events, expanded range and frequency, post-lecture discussions
  • Address integration challenges for new members post-COVID
3 Strengthening Infrastructure
  • Physical: Advocate for improved facilities beyond 60 Turner Street
  • Technological: Enhance digital tools and platforms for learning
  • Organizational: Improve communication systems and community coordination
4 Ensuring Financial Stability
  • Goal: Continue in good financial health into the future
  • Support organizational growth and renewal
  • Enable program innovations and expansions

Mission Statement

"The mission of BOLLI is to promote a welcoming, congenial, and stimulating experience for lifelong learners. This mission is accomplished through exceptional teaching, intellectually enriching courses, and a variety of cultural and social activities."

Vision: To create a member-driven community in which academic excellence, transformational experiences, and meaningful connections thrive, aligned with Brandeis University's ideals of inclusion, truth, and justice.
