CBS, Murrow, and the Commercialization of News

John's Visualization: What Happens When News Outlets Shift Right

  • CBS was the institution built by Edward R. Murrow — famed for See It Now, the McCarthy takedown, and principled journalism
  • David Ellison completed his acquisition of CBS and installed Barry Weiss (Bari Weiss) with a directive to shift toward right-leaning, pro-Trump content
  • Result: CBS news viewership has fallen sharply under the new direction
  • ABC comparator: Trump demanded Kimmel be removed; ABC complied, ratings "plummeted"; Kimmel returned and has since been relentlessly and vocally critical of Trump
  • Washington Post: Bezos directed a tonal shift away from traditional Post standards — drastic decline in readership followed
Weiss quote John found "particularly egregious": "The goal is not to deliver the news so much as it is to derive the news."

John's Free-Market Interpretation

The audience rejection of right-shifted outlets is the free market working. Not government action, not legislation — consumer behavior is pushing back. In an odd way, this is a form of free enterprise operating through public access preferences, driving media in a more progressive direction.
George's Counter: CBS's primary concern may not be ratings — it may be squelching news negative to the Trump administration. John acknowledged both motivations can coexist.
Michael Berger's generational framing: Only people "like us" still read newspapers. The intergenerational gap is real. MSNBC serves the left; Fox serves the right. CBS trying to carve a market that may not exist. ABC doing better because it has strong non-news programming.
Linda Fritz on media ownership: Bezos doesn't just own the Post — he owns Amazon, Whole Foods, and Blue Origin. The Post's tone shift reflects his need to protect those larger business interests, not just newspaper economics.
Murrow historical footnote (John): Even Bill Paley at CBS got "stomach aches" from Murrow's reporting, particularly the McCarthy piece. After that broadcast's success, Paley began to sideline Murrow into celebrity-interview formats Murrow hated. News organizations have always been businesses.

📚 "The Revenge of the Literati" — AI and the Liberal Arts Reversal

John's Counterintuitive Finding

The Unanticipated Role Reversal

John had recently read — and built a portal visualization around — a striking trend: AI is driving computer science and programming jobs down, while simultaneously driving demand for people with liberal arts degrees up. He presented this to the group as a significant and largely unreported story.
  • U.S. programmer employment fell approximately 27.5% between 2023 and 2025 (Stanford Digital Economy Lab / IEEE Spectrum)
  • Software developer employment among ages 22–25 down nearly 20% — entry-level coding is being automated
  • Fine arts graduates now have lower unemployment rates than computer engineers
  • IBM AI chief Matt Candy, Nvidia's Jensen Huang, and Goldman Sachs's George Lee have all publicly said: master language, not coding
  • Cognizant CEO (350,000 workers): AI will create more entry-level jobs for liberal arts grads than for CS grads

Why? The Prompt-as-Research-Proposal Argument

John's explanation to the group: "People who are educated in history and literature know how to read text and compare them — they have a sensibility for uncovering an issue or understanding it. Programmers know how to put that into practice. But now with AI and the prompting tool, you can cut out that technical layer. An unexpected possibility is that people who are in the liberal arts, if they get skilled in using AI, will become the future desirable commodity on the job market over the traditional computer science graduate."
George's immediate reaction: "God." — and then: "John, it goes to show how, for a college student, this could do assignments for them." (Prompting a brief aside about academic integrity that John declined to dwell on.)

The Portal Visualization — "The Unanticipated Role Reversal"

A Live Demonstration of the Thesis

John had already built a dedicated HTML visualization on this theme for the portal, titled "The Unanticipated Role Reversal" — with inline citations and links to sources including IEEE Spectrum, Fortune, Bar-Ilan University, Higher Ed Dive, and Information Week. The act of creating that visualization using AI to research, synthesize, and present the argument itself illustrates the very point: a person with a research framing and analytical judgment directed AI to produce something a team of programmers might have taken days to build.
Alternative title coined: "The Revenge of the Liberal Arts" — or, as John put it in the portal's email digest teaser, "The Revenge of the Literati." The phrase resonated with the BOLLI audience given the community's deep roots in humanities education.

Key Themes & Strategic Insights

Strategy Without History Is Catastrophe

The Literacy Thesis

The John Curl quote framed the entire session: decisions by leaders who lack historical and literary training tend to be impulsive, strategically illiterate, and ultimately self-defeating. Both the Iran war and domestic governance reflect this pattern.
