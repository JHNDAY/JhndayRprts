1897
Birth in New Albany
Born September 25th to Murry and Maud Falkner in New Albany, Mississippi. Family moves to Oxford in 1902.
1918
World War I Service
Joins Royal Canadian Air Force after being rejected by US Army. Serves 179 days but never sees combat. Changes surname from "Falkner" to "Faulkner".
1925
First Novel: Soldiers' Pay
Publishes debut novel while living in New Orleans' French Quarter, influenced by Sherwood Anderson.
1927-1929
Yoknapatawpha County Begins
Writes "Flags in the Dust" (published as "Sartoris"), his first novel set in fictional Yoknapatawpha County. Marries Estelle Oldham in 1929.
1929
The Sound and the Fury
Publishes his experimental masterpiece, written in stream-of-consciousness style. Considers it his breakthrough work.
1930
As I Lay Dying
Completes novel while working night shifts at University of Mississippi Power House. Demonstrates his mastery of multiple narrative voices.
1932-1954
Hollywood Years
Works as screenwriter for MGM and other studios. Contributes to "To Have and Have Not" and "The Big Sleep" with Howard Hawks.
1932
Light in August
Publishes another major novel exploring themes of race, religion, and violence in the American South.
1936
Absalom, Absalom!
Releases what many consider his greatest novel, a complex meditation on Southern history and the burden of the past.
1949
Nobel Prize in Literature
Awarded "for his powerful and artistically unique contribution to the modern American novel." Becomes only Mississippi-born Nobel laureate.
1951 & 1955
National Book Awards
Wins for "Collected Stories" (1951) and "A Fable" (1955).
1954 & 1962
Pulitzer Prizes
Wins for "A Fable" (1954) and posthumously for "The Reivers" (1962).
1957-1958
Writer-in-Residence
Serves as first Writer-in-Residence at University of Virginia, Charlottesville.
1962
Death
Dies July 6th at age 64 from heart attack following a fall from his horse. Buried in St. Peter's Cemetery, Oxford.
