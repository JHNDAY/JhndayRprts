Alex Pretti: VA Nurse Killed by Border Patrol in Minneapolis ▶
ICU Nurse Shot 10 Times During Anti-ICE Protest
On January 24, 2026, Alex Jeffrey Pretti, a 37-year-old intensive care nurse for the US Department of Veterans Affairs, was shot multiple times and killed by US Customs and Border Protection agents in Minneapolis. Wikipedia and PBS News reported Pretti was filming law enforcement with his phone and directing traffic when he was surrounded by agents.
10 shots
Fired in less than 5 seconds
ABC News conducted forensic audio analysis showing 10 shots were fired in less than five seconds. Video recordings verified by Reuters, BBC, Wall Street Journal, and AP showed Pretti standing between an agent and a woman the agent had pushed, putting his arm around the woman before being pepper-sprayed and wrestled to the ground.
"The nation's nurses, who make it their mission to care for and save human lives, are horrified and outraged that immigration agents have once again committed cold-blooded murder of a public observer who posed no threat to them."
— National Nurses United statement
Sources: Wikipedia, PBS News, ABC News
Investigation: Accidental Discharge May Have Triggered Shooting
Fox News reported on January 27 that DHS is investigating whether an accidental discharge of Pretti's own firearm, after it was taken from him by an agent, may have led other agents to believe they were under fire.
Key Details from Investigation:
  • Pretti legally carried a Sig Sauer P320 pistol
  • Weapon was taken from him by agents before shooting
  • Accidental discharge occurred while in agent's possession
  • Sources say agents thought they were being fired upon
  • Footage shows agent disarmed Pretti shortly before he was killed
"None of those agents should've had their gun out. It's a confluence of them being asked to do enforcement work that they are not trained for."
— Source familiar with investigation, New York Post
Sources: Fox News
Who Was Alex Pretti: Green Bay Native, ICU Nurse, Union Advocate
Wisconsin Public Radio and Minnesota Public Radio reported that Pretti was born in Park Ridge, IL, grew up in Green Bay, WI, and graduated from Preble High School in 2006.
Background:
  • University of Minnesota graduate (Biology, Society and Environment, 2011)
  • VA clinical research assistant since 2014
  • Registered nurse in Minnesota since 2021
  • ICU nurse at Minneapolis VA Health Care System
  • Member of American Federation of Government Employees (AFGE)
  • Green Bay Boy Choir member and former Boy Scout
"He cared about people deeply and he was very upset with what was happening in Minneapolis and throughout the United States with ICE, as millions of other people are upset."
— Michael Pretti, Alex's father
Sources: Wisconsin Public Radio, MPR News
Contradicting Narratives: DHS Claims vs. Video Evidence
Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem initially claimed Pretti "violently resisted" arrest and "brandished" a gun at officers. The Washington Post reported that Pretti's family "sharply disputed the Trump administration's version of the shooting."
"There has been no evidence that NPR has verified of Pretti brandishing his handgun at any time during the encounter with federal agents."
— NPR reporting, January 24, 2026
Foundation for Individual Rights and Expression (FIRE) stated: "Trump administration officials have shown little interest in waiting for that process to unfold. Instead, they have rushed to defend the shooting and lock in a narrative that portrays Pretti as someone who wanted to murder law enforcement."
Sources: Washington Post, NPR, FIRE
Immediate Aftermath: Candlelight Vigils and $1M GoFundMe
Wikipedia documented that thousands attended candlelight vigils across the Twin Cities on the night of the shooting. A GoFundMe fundraiser for Pretti's family reached $1,000,000 by January 25, 2026.
On January 26, protesters gathered outside a hotel where ICE agents were staying. About an hour into the protest, several demonstrators entered the hotel vestibule while others threw objects at windows and vandalized the building with graffiti.
Sources: Wikipedia
Renée Good: First Minneapolis Homicide of 2026 ▶
Mother of Three Shot in Her Car by ICE Agent
On January 7, 2026, Renée Nicole Macklin Good, a 37-year-old American woman, was fatally shot in Minneapolis by ICE agent Jonathan Ross. Wikipedia and ABC News reported that Good was in her Honda Pilot stopped sideways in the street when Ross circled her vehicle and fired three shots as she began moving forward.
399 milliseconds
Between first and second gunshots
ABC News metadata analysis showed 399 milliseconds between the first two shots and 299 milliseconds between the second and third shots. Video showed Good turning her steering wheel to the right, away from the agent, just over one second before the first shot.
"Having seen the video myself, I want to tell everybody directly that is bullshit" and "To ICE, get the fuck out of Minneapolis."
— Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey, responding to DHS claims Good tried to run over agent
Sources: Wikipedia, ABC News
Only Minneapolis Homicide in First Week of 2026
Snopes verified that Good's shooting was the only recorded homicide in Minneapolis from January 1-7, 2026. The Minneapolis Police Department confirmed "no shootings in 2026 recorded in Minneapolis prior to Jan. 7, 2026."
Wikipedia noted that Good's killing was the ninth time in five states and Washington, D.C., that ICE agents had opened fire on people since September 2025. Four other people had been killed during federal deportation operations before Good.
Sources: Snopes, Wikipedia
Who Was Renée Good: English Graduate, Poet, Devoted Mother
NBC News and CNN reported that Good graduated from Old Dominion University in Norfolk, Virginia, in December 2020 with a degree in English.
"Renee was one of the kindest people I've ever known. She was extremely compassionate. She's taken care of people all her life. She was loving, forgiving and affectionate. She was an amazing human being."
— Donna Ganger, Renée's mother, to Minnesota Star Tribune
Good had three children including a 6-year-old son she had just dropped off at school before encountering ICE agents. She lived in the Twin Cities with her partner Becca Good and had recently moved from Canada.
Sources: NBC News, CNN
Officer's Body Camera Footage Raises Training Questions
NPR obtained video from ICE officer Jonathan Ross showing him circling Good's vehicle while filming with his cellphone in one hand and holding his gun in the other.
"Now that we can see he's holding a gun in one hand and a cellphone in the other filming, I want to see the officer training that permits that."
— Geoff Alpert, criminology professor, University of South Carolina
"If you are an officer who views this woman as a threat, you don't have one hand on a cellphone. You don't walk around this supposed weapon, casually filming."
— John P. Gross, professor, University of Wisconsin Law School
Sources: NPR
Immediate Response: School Closures and Governor's Proclamation
Minnesota Public Radio reported that Minneapolis Public Schools canceled classes for the rest of the week as a precautionary measure, offering an online option through February 12.
Minnesota Governor Tim Walz proclaimed January 9, 2026, to be "Renee Good Day." Wikipedia reported that Politico documented the killing kicked off efforts by state legislators nationwide to limit cooperation with and increase oversight of ICE.
Sources: MPR News, Wikipedia

ICE Deaths in Custody: At Least Six Already in 2026
Al Jazeera reported on January 27 that at least six immigrants have died in ICE custody already in 2026, with a seventh person fatally shot by an off-duty ICE officer. Last year, 32 deaths were reported in ICE custody.
The report documented that while most deaths were due to health complications, some detainees' families have made accusations of abuse and medical neglect against ICE.
Sources: Al Jazeera
Minnesota National Guard Activated
PBS News reported that Governor Tim Walz activated the Minnesota National Guard to assist local police amid growing protests. Guard troops were deployed both to shooting sites and to the federal Whipple Building where officials squared off with protesters daily.
"Minnesotans and our local law enforcement have done everything we can to deescalate. The federal government must deescalate. I once again call on the President to remove the 3,000 agents from Minnesota who are sowing chaos and violence."
— Governor Tim Walz
Sources: PBS News
Bipartisan Congressional Response & Political Fallout ▶
Republicans Join Democrats in Calling for Investigations
NPR reported on January 26 that "a handful of federal Republican lawmakers are publicly calling for independent, transparent or joint state-and-federal investigations into Pretti's shooting."
Republican Senators Criticizing ICE Actions:
  • Tom Tillis (R-NC): Called for investigation
  • Bill Cassidy (R-LA): Expressed concerns
  • Thomas Massie (R-KY): Spoke out against tactics
Rep. Andrew Garbarino (R-NY), chair of the committee overseeing DHS, sent a letter requesting three top DHS officials appear for questioning before the committee.
Sources: NPR
Democratic Calls for ICE Abolition and Noem Impeachment
PBS News reported that Congressman Robert Garcia, the top Democrat on the House Oversight Committee, called for ICE to be "abolished" and for Homeland Security Secretary Kristi Noem to be impeached.
"Trump has created a militarized police force accountable only to him and ready to murder people in our streets."
— Rep. Robert Garcia (D-CA)
Reps. Delia Ramirez (D-IL) and Seth Magaziner (D-RI) co-sponsored the DHS Use of Force Oversight Act to establish a baseline policy for use of force.
Sources: PBS News, NPR
Senate Democrats Block DHS Funding
Senate Democrats took action to block Department of Homeland Security funding in response to the killings, forcing the issue back to the House for another vote. The move represented one of the few legislative tools available to the minority party to express opposition to ICE operations.

Historical Parallels: Emmett Till and Kent State
Media commentary and political discussions drew comparisons between Alex Pretti's killing and two watershed moments in American history: Emmett Till's murder in 1955 and the Kent State shootings in 1970.
Historical Comparisons:
  • Emmett Till (1955): Mother's open-casket funeral changed national conscience on race
  • Kent State (1970): National Guard shooting of students galvanized anti-war movement
  • Alex Pretti (2026): Question: "Are we witnessing the moment when enough people say not one more?"
Analysts noted key differences: Unlike Kent State victims, Pretti was a grown man who chose to be at the protest. Unlike Till, Pretti was white and a military veteran. But the central question remained: Would this become the tipping point that makes the political cost of the status quo unbearable?
