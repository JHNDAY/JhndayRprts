August 26 Focus Group

In person. 9 attendees plus two moderators. Emphasis on "newish" members who joined BOLLI since Covid. Consequently, they were not familiar with time when everything was in person ‚Äì classes, lunch time speakers, etc. Participants included one person who will teach in the fall, one SIG chair and one person who led a summer test kitchen program.

What people appreciate about BOLLI

  • Very welcoming
  • Love in person classes and excursions
  • Richness of curriculum
  • Looked at Tufts and UMass Boston but BOLLI much better program
  • Taylor Ackley in person
  • Excellent support for SGLs ‚Äì comment from somebody who is a new SGL

Negatives

  • Not enough diversity
  • Curriculum too Jewish - two people. Others appreciate "unique experience of courses with topics related to Judaism"
  • Not enough in person classes ‚Äì "learning is a social experience"
  • Too much reliance on volunteers "when I come up with an idea, there is nobody to implement. I'm told to do it myself."
  • Covid created an epidemic of loneliness and isolation; Zoom continues that epidemic
  • Too intellectual ‚Äì one person. Others disagreed
  • Catalog too complicated

Program Ideas

  • With other OLLIs, develop a series of talks on special topics (e.g., 1619 project) and make available to all programs
  • Create "one-offs" on topics of interest, such as Shakespeare
  • More combined experiences, such as the Dartmouth lectures, with other OLLIs
  • Mini-courses
  • Need orientation to understand how things work, e.g., lottery, catalog, number of courses one can take (note that most of these people began at BOLLI for membership committee was formed)
  • Have more than two terms
  • Use experienced faculty to teach SGLs "college teaching techniques"
  • Start a cooking club ‚Äì suggested by somebody who is a professional chef
  • Create SIGs for theater and films
  • Require homework and assignments
  • Add sites on north and south shores, using public spaces such as libraries
  • Bring in more Brandeis faculty
  • Encourage field trips for classes and include field trips in course descriptions
  • More science and math courses
  • Hybrid classes

Marketing ideas

(participants commented that they were not aware of any marketing efforts currently)

  • Outreach to 55 plus communities
  • Flyers at senior centers and libraries
  • Develop promotional materials
  • Take CAST on tour to synagogues and use as an opportunity to make people aware of BOLLI
