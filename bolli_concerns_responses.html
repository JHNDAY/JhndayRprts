Strategic Response Framework

How BOLLI's Priority Objectives Address Key Concerns from Strategic Planning

Post-COVID Impact
Major Concern
Loss of Community Connection
Members who joined post-COVID often never met classmates or SGLs in person. Difficulty maintaining community feeling when people can't meet face-to-face. Community events eliminated or moved online.
Program Enhancement
Strategic Response
Community Building Initiatives
  • Designate weekly community time (Lunch and Learn)
  • Make nametags more visible and available
  • Support member-formed SIGs with guidance
  • Experiment with evening/geographic meet-ups
  • Restore member directory
  • Expand new member orientation with mentoring
Volunteer Crisis
Major Concern
Declining Member SGL Participation
More classes taught by non-members than members. Member SGLs "retiring" without replacements. Difficulty recruiting leadership committee members due to reduced loyalty and commitment.
Infrastructure & Program
Strategic Response
Comprehensive Volunteer Revitalization
  • Survey membership for teaching backgrounds and skills
  • Expand workbook and panel sessions for SGLs
  • Recruit retiring faculty from schools/colleges
  • Integrate volunteer opportunities in new member orientation
  • Increase volunteer incentives and social occasions
Financial Sustainability
Major Concern
Revenue and Cost Pressures
High fees compared to peers. Cost of external SGLs receiving stipends. Pressure from Brandeis to reduce expenses. Weak history of fundraising. No strategic plan for 20 years.
Financial Sustainability
Strategic Response
Comprehensive Revenue Strategy
  • Form Marketing Committee to expand membership
  • Target marketing at senior centers and colleges
  • Re-engage past/trial members
  • Form Development/Fundraising Committee
  • Target specific fundraising campaign (new space)
  • Improve social media and targeted advertising
Infrastructure
Major Concern
Completely Inadequate Space & Tech
Basement location, no accessibility for stairs. No windows, no flexibility. Poor IT support, can't record classes, lack hybrid infrastructure. Offices not near classrooms.
Infrastructure
Strategic Response
Space & Technology Transformation
  • Plan buildout of 2nd floor at 29 Sawyer
  • Clarify options for 51 Sawyer
  • Explore other Brandeis campus possibilities
  • Implement hybrid technology with built-in systems
  • Consult organizations with hybrid best practices
Program Limitations
Major Concern
Curriculum Gaps & Schedule Rigidity
Lack of how-to and lifestyle courses. Rigidity of 5 and 10 week daytime schedule. Random curriculum generation. Requests for à la carte pricing. Poor website quality.
Program Enhancement
Strategic Response
Flexible Programming & Curriculum Planning
  • Pilot courses outside usual schedule (evening, 2-4 week)
  • Develop à la carte pricing models
  • Survey members about desired subject areas
  • Partner with art centers for courses
  • Use AI to redesign catalog (searchable, calendar format)
  • Create YouTube channel with course previews
  • Improvements to website and catalog
  • Recruit outside SGLs to fill gaps in programming
