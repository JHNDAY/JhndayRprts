  • Online courses average 25.8 total student choices per course vs 23.1 for in-person courses
  • Online courses are significantly more popular as first choices (7.6 avg vs 5.4 avg)
  • In-person courses interestingly perform better as second choices (6.6 avg vs 5.2 avg for online)
  • Both categories show similar overall student engagement levels per course

Online vs In-Person Preference

  • Online courses are significantly more popular, receiving 69.6% of all choices (1,005 out of 1,443)
  • In-Person courses received 30.4% of all choices (438 out of 1,443)

Choice Priority Patterns

Online Courses:

  • Most popular as first choice (298 selections)
  • Second choices drop to 202, then recover slightly to 214 third choices
  • Strong consistent demand across all choice levels

In-Person Courses:

  • Interesting pattern: second choices (126) are more popular than first choices (102)
  • Third choices drop significantly to 59 selections
  • May indicate these courses are often backup options
