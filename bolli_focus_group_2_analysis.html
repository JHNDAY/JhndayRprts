Member Experiences at BOLLI

Standout Learning Experiences
I started in BOLLI even before I retired... and I think I was born to be a teacher. Actually, I played teacher when I was a kid. So this program appealed to me on 2 levels, one, that I could learn a lot, and one that I could teach.

Valued experiences:

Pre-Retirement Engagement Teaching Opportunities Academic Rigor Diverse Course Choices
Mixed Course Experiences
I was trying to think how long I've been at BOLLI, and I think it's probably 20 years or so... I've never had in all those years a bad course.
I have had probably 4 or 5 classes I've been pretty disappointed in. Only once did I literally not finish it. But you know, and I think that was either the teacher style or they just weren't good teachers, or, you know, wasn't a good match for me.

Factors affecting course satisfaction:

  • Teaching style compatibility
  • Instructor experience and skill
  • Personal learning preferences
  • Course design and expectations
Multi-Program Experiences
I have participated in outside and other OLLI's, most notably the one in Sarasota where we winter. So I've taken classes there, and I've seen how another program works and has a very different approach.

Comparison programs mentioned:

Sarasota OLLI UMass OLLI Johns Hopkins Osher Temple Israel Program Harvard ILR
Comparative Program Analysis
Classes are different lengths, some are 6 weeks, some are 5 weeks, some are 4 weeks, some are 2 days... No preparation required. so people would just come in and listen and ask questions and leave the room.

Other programs' advantages:

  • Flexible Scheduling: Staggered course lengths and start times
  • Evening Options: Some programs offer classes for working professionals
  • Lower Barriers: No preparation required for some courses
  • Social Integration: Temple Israel includes lunch and speakers in registration
  • Pricing Flexibility: UMass OLLI allows single course enrollment

BOLLI's comparative strengths:

  • Academic Rigor: Reading assignments and preparation enhance learning
  • Interactive Discussion: Cross-talk and peer engagement
  • Course Variety: "Best catalog every semester in terms of variety and content"
  • Community Building: Name recognition and member connections
  • No Admission Requirements: Unlike Harvard ILR's interview process

Zoom vs. In-Person

Pro-In-Person Arguments
I wish I could give 10 votes or 200 votes to the on-site in-person class. The conviviality... the getting together with people and the sense of community... more active learning takes place when you're in a room.

In-person advantages cited:

Community Building Cross-Discussion Active Engagement Natural Socialization Better Participation Flow
Pro-Zoom Perspectives
I have to express a divergent opinion, because no one else has so far. I'm very happy that there are so many zoom options... I would probably possibly not continue with BOLLI if there were. If Zoom was eliminated or minimized a lot.

Zoom advantages cited:

Transportation Independence Accessibility Caregiving Compatibility Weather Independence Recorded Sessions
The Supply-Side Reality
The limiting factor is the number of people who are willing to teach in person... we have not been able to convince people to come back to in person after zoom... it's just really, really hard to get people to teach in in person classes.

Instructor challenges:

  • Caregiving responsibilities
  • Transportation difficulties
  • Health considerations
  • Convenience preferences
  • Technology comfort

Key Challenges Identified

Physical Facility Issues
I think Turner is absolutely terrible. I don't worry about window dressing. I worry about safety. When that elevator was out... There's no easy egress in an emergency.
Safety Concerns: Basement location with limited emergency exits, especially problematic when elevator is out of service
Acoustics: Poor sound quality in central room with noise bleeding between classrooms
Layout Problems: Circular classroom configuration limiting visibility and discussion
Course Access and Lottery System
This is the second time where I haven't had a full assignment of courses... I had a list of 10 requests. I got into none of them.
Lottery Confusion: System appears opaque and difficult to understand for many members
High-Demand Instructors: Popular teachers create access bottlenecks
Underenrolled Courses: Some classes struggle to fill while others are oversubscribed
Existence of Course Advisors Not Known or Understood: People struggled to select courses and were unhappy with lottery results
Community Connection Challenges
I feel BOLLI has evolved away from fostering a sense of community, and I miss that... it just doesn't have the same warm feeling that it did many years ago.
Missing Elements: Name tags not prominently available, member directory discontinued
Post-COVID Changes: Loss of daily shared meals and speakers
Zoom Limitations: Reduced casual interactions and relationship building

Vision for BOLLI's Future

Flexible Membership Models
I'd love to see a few more options based on how much you want to participate in the program... why am I paying all this money? And I'm not getting what I want.
Tiered Pricing: Options for single courses, partial enrollment, or full membership
Evening Classes: Accommodate working professionals and create more variety
Staggered Schedules: Different course lengths and start times like UMass OLLI model
Enhanced Course Delivery
I wonder if somebody would be willing to run the same course twice, either on different days or back to back... particularly if we gave them a stipend.
Instructor Incentives: Small stipends to encourage in-person teaching
Better Course Guidance: Improve advisor system and member testimonials
Community Building Initiatives
Maybe BOLLI could maybe come up with 2 times during a week where it's very much specified. It's come, have lunch. Let's just chat... every week on Friday at 12 to one.
Regular Social Hours: Structured weekly community time
Geographic Meetups: Local gatherings for members in similar areas
Hybrid Discussion Groups: Post-lecture discussions for Distinguished Speaker events
Program Expansion Ideas
Hands-On Learning: More practical courses like drawing, conversation groups, crafts
Partnership Opportunities: Collaborate with local art centers and cultural institutions
Transportation Solutions: Carpools, van service, or central meeting points for excursions
Younger Member Outreach: Marketing to attract early retirees and part-time workers
