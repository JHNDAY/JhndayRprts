Safari Split Screen
on MacBook Air

Three ways to view two pages side by side

01
macOS Split View Full-screen split — best for focused work
← hover here
Safari
Other App
  1. 1Open a Safari window
  2. 2Hover over the green ⬤ button (top-left) — don't click, just hover
  3. 3Choose "Tile Window to Left of Screen" or right from the popup menu
  4. 4Click a second window on the opposite side to fill it
  5. 5Drag the center divider to resize each panel
💡 To exit: hover the green button again → "Make Window Full Screen", or press Esc
or
02
Two Windows, Side by Side Freeform desktop layout — stays out of full-screen mode
  1. 1Open Safari
  2. 2Go to File → New Window or press ⌘N
  3. 3Manually drag and resize each window to fill half the screen
✅ Works on all macOS versions. Simple and always available — no special menu needed.
or
03
Safari Tab Split View Native split inside one Safari window — macOS Sonoma (14)+ only
  1. 1In Safari, right-click any tab in the tab bar
  2. 2Select "Move to Split View" from the context menu
  3. 3A second pane opens alongside your current tab — all within one Safari window
⚠️ Requires macOS Sonoma (14) or later. Check your version: Apple Menu → About This Mac.

⌨️ Handy Tips

Mission Control Press ^ ↑ to see all open windows before arranging a split.
Resize the Divider In any split view, drag the center bar left or right to adjust how much space each side gets.
Swap Sides Click and drag the title bar of either panel in macOS Split View to swap left and right.
Check Your macOS Apple Menu → About This Mac to confirm your version and unlock Method 3 if available.
