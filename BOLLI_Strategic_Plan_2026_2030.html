Who We Are

A vibrant, welcoming, member-driven community of intellectually engaged adults

The Brandeis Osher Lifelong Learning Institute (BOLLI) is a vibrant, welcoming, member-driven community of intellectually engaged adults based at Brandeis University in Waltham, Massachusetts. Founded in 2000 with support from the Bernard Osher Foundation, BOLLI offers non-credit courses, lectures, discussion groups, and shared interest groups (SIGs) designed for adults who love to learn.


BOLLI members are both learners and leaders‚Äîcreating and teaching courses, forming SIGs, and shaping the organization through volunteerism. Courses span literature, history, politics, science, art, and current events, available both in-person and online, enabling members to stay active, connected, and intellectually stimulated.

Why Plan Now

Celebrating 25 years and planning for the next phase of growth and renewal

As BOLLI celebrates its 25th anniversary, the time is right to plan for the next phase of growth and renewal. Member needs and expectations are evolving, influenced by new technologies, generational change, and post-pandemic realities of hybrid learning.

This Strategic Plan Will:

  • Clarify priorities for sustaining and growing membership
  • Strengthen BOLLI's organizational and financial foundation
  • Foster innovation in teaching and learning
  • Deepen community connection
  • Keep BOLLI aligned with the broader ideals of Brandeis University: inclusion, truth, and justice

Goal 1: Enhance the BOLLI Curriculum

Academically-oriented curriculum with balance between in-person and Zoom

BOLLI community members support the value of an academically-oriented curriculum, which distinguishes our program from others in the Boston area and nationally. A balance between in-person and Zoom is highly valued. Members also expressed a desire for additions to the curriculum beyond current offerings.

Intentionally Plan Curriculum

We will issue RFPs internally for desired courses and recruit external instructors, prioritizing Brandeis faculty, to fill gaps.

Increase Participation of Member SGLs

Until Covid, peer teaching was the norm. Some of BOLLI's most popular courses are led by members. To continue this tradition, we will intensify efforts to expand the number of peer SGLs through active recruitment, expanded training, mentorship and encouraging co-teaching.

Experiment with Flexible Scheduling

We will continue to pilot mini-courses and expand summer/winter session activities. Evening classes and courses of different lengths will be initiated.

Increase and Support In-Person Classes

Members who prefer to take in-person classes have fewer choices than those who prefer Zoom. To achieve a better balance, we will actively recruit Brandeis faculty and external instructors for on-site courses to supplement in-person member SGLs.

Enlarge the Toolbox for SGLs

While SGLs have become quite proficient with Zoom, additional assistance will allow instructors to concentrate on content. Initiatives will include a new cohort of trained co-hosts, support for digital literacy and instruction in AI tools, as well as other emerging technologies.

Add Non-Traditional Courses

Through a subcommittee of the Curriculum Committee, we will research other lifelong learning programs for new courses that would appeal to our community. In addition, we will partner with local cultural institutions for experiential learning (such as theater, art, and film organizations).

Expand Partnerships with Other Osher Programs

We will continue to publicize open courses from other OLLIs, partner in joint lecture series and pilot shared courses and post-lecture discussions.

Goal 2: Strengthen the BOLLI Community

Building connections beyond the classroom

BOLLI members hail the importance of being part of a "learning community" that goes beyond participating in interesting classes. Observations are that it can be difficult for new members to integrate into the community, especially post-covid, and that BOLLI can be a confusing place. Members feel that participation in committees or projects can help to make connections and gain deeper satisfaction.

Emphasize Community in All Messaging

We will promote "BOLLI as a community of learners" in all materials and expand orientation and mentorship for new and newer members.

Draw Members for Social and Informal Programs

To stimulate informal interactions before and between classes and other in-person events, we will reinstate food and coffee hours, lunches, name tags, and weekly informal gatherings.

Use Classroom Experiences to Build Connection

We will encourage field trips, breakout sessions, and social components in courses and support SGLs in offering "get-acquainted" sessions before the start of a class.

Revitalize Volunteerism

BOLLI needs volunteers to provide the creative and sustaining framework for the operation of our programs. We will broaden volunteer opportunities; celebrate and train volunteers; integrate volunteering into orientation, and promote committee and special project participation as a way to feel more connected to BOLLI.

Enhance Distinguished Speaker Series (DSS)

We will pilot hybrid events, expand speaker range and frequency, and offer refreshments and small-group discussions following larger lectures.

Encourage SIG Formation and Engagement

We will provide additional guidance and support for the shared interest groups, as well as to members considering starting a new SIG and promote "off-hours" and offsite gatherings, when appropriate.

Improve Internal Communication

We will restore the member directory, clarify the lottery process, and enhance the BOLLI Bulletin as a hub of information sharing.

Improve Website, Catalog, and Lottery System

We will form a new working group to oversee redesign of our website and explore implementation of a searchable course catalog.

Goal 3: Improve Physical, Technological, and Organizational Infrastructure

Creating the foundation for excellence

BOLLI community members expressed deep concern about the inadequacy of the 60 Turner Street space for many reasons: size of classrooms, poor climate control, lack of windows, insufficient audio systems for hearing-challenged members, and the basement location, among other concerns. These shortcomings hamper BOLLI's ability to offer more community events and in-person classes. Concerns were also expressed about technological support for members and SGLs and lack of hybrid classes and lectures.

Move to a New Space

Brandeis is our home and our landlord. As the university explores acquisition of new property, they have prioritized a new home for BOLLI. We will continue to collaborate with Brandeis leadership in planning for new space that is appropriate to our needs.

Implement Hybrid Technology

New space will be equipped with technology to support hybrid learning. In the interim, we will pilot hybrid classes beginning in spring 2026, using appropriate equipment and best practices. In addition, we will make audio recordings available for all in-person classes.

Improve Technology Literacy

Most continuing SGLs are now well versed in Zoom but we continue to experience bumps with new people and, in isolated incidences, with continuing SGLs. Some members still have difficulty in the Zoom environment. We will initiate more support, through workshops and personal training, for SGLs and BOLLI members.

Goal 4: Ensure Financial Sustainability

Building a strong financial foundation for the future

Many of the objectives in the strategic plan require financial resources and the ability to understand and track our finances. BOLLI community members raised some concerns about the cost of BOLLI membership. Others commented that we should emphasize that tuition includes more than courses, such as shared interest groups, the distinguished speaker series, between term programs (Winter/Summer), and arts and culture excursions. While we understand that tuition is high, we concluded that separate pricing for courses would undermine the mission of BOLLI as a learning community. Enhancing our fundraising capacity will allow BOLLI to offer a more robust scholarship program.

Forge a Closer Relationship with Brandeis

We will align more closely with Institutional Advancement to encourage alumni participation, consider a joint travel program, and move forward on other efforts in which collaboration is beneficial. We will also explore further associations with cultural institutions on the Brandeis campus.

Strengthen Financial Oversight

We have begun to develop stronger relationships with Brandeis administrative offices that will aid in a better understanding of our finances and Brandeis administrative charges. An important goal is to develop a strategic use of our endowment and gift funds, as well as philanthropic needs, so that we can build a strong footing for our future.

Expand Marketing and Membership Recruitment

Our enrollment has been steady at slightly under 500 members. Given our emphasis on providing a high quality experience, we believe that this is the appropriate size for BOLLI. In order to maintain a stable enrollment, we will convene a Marketing Committee to re-engage past members and develop a multi-channel outreach plan to attract new members.

Ramp Up Fundraising

We will form a Philanthropy Committee and launch an annual giving campaign. We will also begin targeted fundraising efforts for scholarships, teaching, technology upgrades and special programs.

BOLLI's success over the past 25 years rests on its members' creativity, intellectual curiosity, and commitment to community. This strategic plan outlines a course for the next chapter‚Äîexpanding opportunities for learning and connection, strengthening organizational resilience, and ensuring that BOLLI continues to thrive as a model of lifelong learning within the Brandeis tradition.
