Radical Jewish Philosophies: A Legacy of Spinoza

Traces contemporary radical Jewish philosophies back to Spinoza's 17th-century critique. Through systematic examination of God, Torah, and Israel across centuries (Rabbinic period, Maimonides, Spinoza, modern movements), students see how Jewish ideas evolve with social change.

Close reading of Sarah Moss's memoir illuminates anorexia's psychological, medical, sociological dimensions. Examines early signs, family dynamics, life-cycle triggers, social pressures, treatment spectrum, chronicity, recovery, and memoir as therapeutic education.
