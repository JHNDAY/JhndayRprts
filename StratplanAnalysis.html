‚ö†Ô∏è Significant Gaps in Strategic Plan

1. Cost & Financial Accessibility

Focus Group Concern: "People are leaving because of the cost... we may be pricing ourselves out"

Strategic Plan Gap: While "Cost and Value" appears as a concern in Group 2, there's limited strategic response to pricing flexibility, sliding scale options, or alternative membership models that participants extensively discussed.

2. Physical Plant & Facility Issues

Focus Group Concern: "It's really not good... basement location, poor ventilation, safety concerns, no windows"

Strategic Plan Gap: "Physical Plant Issues" noted in Group 1, but the strategic plan doesn't clearly address facility relocation or major infrastructure improvements as a strategic priority.

3. Website & Information Architecture

Focus Group Concern: "We have the worst website... none of this is intuitive... people don't know about what's being offered"

Strategic Plan Gap: While "Information Gaps" and "Communication Failures" are identified, the strategic plan doesn't prioritize comprehensive website redesign or information architecture overhaul.

4. New Member Integration & Onboarding

Focus Group Concern: "Nobody talked to me... how to make the adaptation smoother... freshman orientation"

Strategic Plan Gap: Community building is recognized, but specific onboarding, mentorship, or new member integration programs aren't clearly articulated as strategic initiatives.

5. Competitive Positioning & Marketing

Focus Group Concern: "Much more competitive now... we're the expensive one... no marketing at all"

Strategic Plan Gap: "Marketing & Visibility" and "Competitive Landscape" are noted concerns, but strategic marketing initiatives and competitive positioning strategies aren't clearly defined.

üìà Overall Assessment

The strategic plan demonstrates good awareness of the major themes emerging from focus groups, particularly around community building, academic quality, and the Zoom/in-person balance. However, there's a significant gap between problem identification and strategic solution development.

The plan would benefit from:

  • More specific, actionable initiatives
  • Clear prioritization and resource allocation
  • Metrics and accountability measures
  • Implementation timelines
  • Address the "low-hanging fruit" issues that could be resolved quickly
