Core Thesis

Innovation ignites not at invention, but at commoditization — when the means of production are cheap enough for entrepreneurial agents to cascade. AI has now crossed that threshold. The API-ization of frontier intelligence is the functional equivalent of cheap photons from a solar panel: the means of cognitive production have been commoditized.

Historical Precedent · Solar PV
The IEA repeatedly underestimated solar penetration because their models couldn't anticipate the entrepreneurial cascade that cheap panels would unleash. Market penetration exceeded all forecasts — not because the technology was magic, but because learning curves were steeper than modeled, and entrepreneurs found applications the forecasters hadn't imagined. Both dynamics appear operative in AI right now.

III. Where the Analogy Holds
Structural match
01
Infrastructure Is Now Separable from Application
As a solar installer need not understand semiconductor physics, an entrepreneur deploying AI-powered legal research need not understand transformer architectures. The abstraction layer is complete enough for mass deployment by non-specialists. This is the crucial democratization moment.
Scope advantage
02
Combinatorial Explosion Across Every Domain
Solar displaces one thing: thermal energy generation. AI potentially displaces or augments the cognitive labor embedded in every sector simultaneously — law, medicine, education, logistics, drug discovery, software engineering itself. The surface area for entrepreneurial attack is historically unprecedented.
Early confirmation
03
The "Surprising" Prediction Is Already Being Validated
GitHub Copilot adoption speed. AI's effective demolition of the bar exam barrier. AlphaFold's resolution of a 50-year protein-folding problem in a single release. These are not incremental improvements — they are phase transitions, arriving faster than even optimistic forecasts. The IEA effect, again.
IV. Where the Analogy Strains — or Gets More Interesting
Crucial asymmetry
01
Solar Converted Energy. AI Converts Agency.
A panel produces electrons that slot into existing infrastructure. AI produces decisions, diagnoses, and code — outputs previously reserved for credentialed human agents. The innovation cascade isn't just new products; it's the restructuring of entire professions. Social friction will be substantially higher, making the timeline non-linear.
Platform risk
02
The "Big Idea" Is Still Becoming Itself
With PV, the physics was settled — the task was engineering and manufacturing scale. With AI, the underlying capability is in rapid, unpredictable development. Entrepreneurs build on a substrate that is itself changing. Products may be deprecated by the next model release — the so-called "getting Anthropic'd" problem.
Topology difference
03
Network Effects and Concentration Risk
Solar's commodity structure is genuinely distributed — anyone can put panels on a roof. AI's commodity layer sits atop a highly concentrated model infrastructure: Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Meta, with China as a parallel track. The entrepreneurial layer is open, but the foundational layer is oligopolistic — a different and more fragile innovation topology.
V. Verdict
Assessment of the Hypothesis
Large?
✓ Confirmed
Almost certainly. General-purpose technologies that cross the mass-production threshold reliably produce productivity gains measured in GDP percentage points over decade-plus horizons. AI's breadth of cognitive application suggests a magnitude potentially exceeding electricity and the internet.
Surprising?
✓ Confirmed — and specifically so
The surprises will not come from current focal points (chatbots, content generation) but from second and third-order applications not yet named. The internet's transformative applications — social media, gig economy, streaming — were not forecast by TCP/IP's architects. The AI equivalents are probably not yet in anyone's pitch deck.
The main caveat: the social and institutional absorption of a cognitive general-purpose technology may be choppier and more contested than an energetic one. But that would delay, not prevent, the predicted cascade. The hypothesis is well-constructed and probably correct in its broad strokes.
