1 Transitioning Between Gallery and Presentation Views

Understanding how Zoom handles different views is essential for seamless course delivery. When you or a participant shares content, Zoom automatically adjusts the layout to prioritize that shared content.

Default Gallery View (No Screen Share Active)

When no one is sharing their screen, Zoom displays all participants in a gallery layout. This is ideal for discussion-based sessions where you want to see everyone's engagement and reactions.

Automatic Transition to Presentation Mode

When screen sharing begins: The shared content automatically becomes the primary focus and fills the main screen area. Participant videos move to a smaller strip at the top of the screen.
Host visibility: As the host, you'll see your own shared content prominently displayed, just as your participants see it. This helps you present more effectively.
To stop sharing: Click the red "Stop Share" button that appears at the top center of your screen. This returns everyone to gallery view.

Manual View Switching

While Zoom automatically adjusts views when screen sharing starts or stops, you can also manually transition between Gallery and Presentation modes at any time. Look for the tabs in the upper left side of the top menu, next to the "Zoom Meeting" label:

  • Meeting tab: Click this to view Gallery View (all participants)
  • Person's Presentation tab: Click this to view the shared content when someone is presenting

This gives you flexibility to switch your own view without affecting what others see or interrupting the screen sharing.

Practice transitioning between views before your first session. Share a simple document or your desktop, then stop sharing, so you're comfortable with the flow. Also experiment with manually clicking between the Meeting and Presentation tabs.
Important: While the transition happens automatically when screen sharing starts or stops, you can always manually override this using the tabs in the upper left corner.

2 Using the View Controls (Upper Right Corner)

The View button in the upper right corner gives you powerful options to customize how you see your meeting, regardless of what others are seeing. These controls are especially useful when you need to adapt to different teaching scenarios.

Accessing View Options

Location

Look for the "View" button in the upper right corner of your Zoom window, next to the minimize and close buttons. It typically shows a grid icon or the word "View".

Available View Modes

Speaker View

  • Highlights the active speaker in a large window
  • Other participants appear in smaller thumbnails
  • Best for: Lecture-style presentations or when one person is primarily speaking
  • Automatically switches focus to whoever is talking

Gallery View

  • Shows all participants in equally-sized tiles
  • Can display up to 49 participants per screen
  • Best for: Interactive discussions and small group engagement
  • Helps you monitor student participation and engagement

When Screen Sharing is Active

You get additional view options when content is being shared:

  • Side-by-Side Mode: Splits your screen between the shared content and participant videos
  • Standard Mode: Shared content fills the screen with participants in a small strip at the top
  • Minimize Participants: Temporarily hide participant videos to focus entirely on shared content
If you're using dual monitors, you can have participants on one screen and shared content on the other. Enable this in Settings > General > "Use dual monitors".

View Preferences That Persist

Remember: Your view choice is personal‚Äîchanging your view doesn't affect what participants see. You can use Speaker View while your students remain in Gallery View, or vice versa.

3 Understanding the Meeting Toolbar

The toolbar at the bottom of your Zoom window contains all the essential meeting controls. Hover over any icon to see its name and function. Here's a comprehensive guide to each button:

üìπ Video (Start/Stop)

üí¨ Chat

Opens the text chat panel. Send messages to everyone or privately to specific participants. Useful for sharing links, collecting questions, or side conversations that don't interrupt the flow.

üòä React

üü¢ Share Screen
