BOLLI Space Planning Analysis

51 Sawyer Building, 5th Floor - Evaluating Campus Planning Options

BOLLI's Core Space Requirements

Three classrooms with at least 17 seats each (seminar-style, not lecture)
Large gathering space for 70+ people (DSS, New Yorker discussions, special events)
Screens in each classroom and large lecture space
Flexible space that can open up for large lectures (30-50 people)
Administrative offices and support spaces
Kitchen/refreshment area for community gatherings

Key Challenge

The current test fit options provide classrooms that are too small (10 and 12 person) and lack the critical large gathering space that BOLLI uses for community functions like Distinguished Speakers Series and New Yorker discussions.

Option 1: Current Test Fit

As presented by Campus Planning

Pros

  • One 18-seat classroom available
  • Nice lounge space
  • Professional office layout
  • Good for small administrative meetings

Cons

  • Two classrooms too small (10 & 12 seats)
  • Third classroom is lecture-style, not seminar
  • No equivalent to current gathering space
  • Special events would need off-site venues
Option 2: Modified Layout

Judy Feldman's suggestion with moveable partition

Pros

  • Moveable partition for flexibility
  • Can combine lounge + classroom for lectures
  • Seminar-style seating in circles
  • Better use of open space

Cons

  • Still limited to 30-50 for large events
  • Need for 70+ person space unresolved
  • Scheduling conflicts between classes and events
Option 3: Oliver's Redesign

"Nuke" an office, relocate kitchen

Pros

  • Convert small classroom to 18-seat room
  • Kitchen becomes office space
  • Wall-mounted kitchen like Turner Street
  • More efficient space utilization

Cons

  • Lose dedicated office space
  • Still doesn't solve 70+ gathering need
  • Requires significant renovation
Option 4: Hybrid Approach

Combining on-floor space with building resources

Pros

  • Use larger downstairs space for big events
  • Optimize 5th floor for daily operations
  • Access to additional adjacent space
  • Remove walls to expand into vacated areas
  • Cost-effective solution

Cons

  • Event setup/breakdown logistics
  • Loss of spontaneous community gathering
  • Potential scheduling conflicts with building
  • Less cohesive BOLLI community space
