Step 3 Setting up the Meeting

6-7 Create Meeting
  1. Click on "Meetings" on the top left of the screen
  2. Use the blue-outlined button on the top right: "Schedule a Meeting"
8-12 Basic Meeting Configuration
Topic: Fill in the name of your Class/Special Interest Group
Date & Duration: Leave the same (doesn't matter for recurring meetings)
Time Zone: Set your time zone
Recurring meeting: Fill in a name for your recurring meeting. You may use name of your class/SIG, or you may use your own name if you will use this Zoom link for more than one class.
Recurrence: Choose "No Fixed Time" from dropdown
üîë IMPORTANT: USE THIS LINK FOR YOUR TEST MEETING AND ALL CLASS/SIG MEETINGS. THERE IS NO NEED TO GENERATE A NEW LINK EACH WEEK AS LONG AS YOU CHOOSE RECURRING AND NO FIXED TIME.

Step 4 Obtain Your Host Key

23 Getting Your Host Key
  1. On the left hand side, click the Profile tab (above the Meetings tab)
  2. Scroll down to the Meeting section
  3. Click the eyeball icon in the Host Key row
  4. Write down your Host Key (a six digit number)
  5. Keep it handy (maybe on a yellow sticky note)
üí° About Host Keys:
  • You may change your Host Key to any 6-digit number you prefer
  • The Host Key allows you to become a Host in your meeting by clicking your link, if you can't log in the normal way
  • See Step 25 for how to use it in emergency situations

Step 5 Starting the Meeting

24 Proper Way to Start Your Meeting
‚úÖ CORRECT METHOD:
  1. Go to brandeis.zoom.us/signin
  2. Login to your account
  3. Find the meeting
  4. Click START or JOIN (if others are already in the meeting)
‚ùå DO NOT:
  • Click HOST A MEETING in the upper right of your screen
  • Click on the link from an email

If you do either of these, you will not be the host!

25 Emergency Host Access
üÜò IF YOU ARE UNABLE TO LOG IN TO YOUR MEETING, DO NOT PANIC:
  1. Click the link you provided to your class
  2. Join the meeting as a participant
  3. Once in, click the Participants icon in the bottom row
  4. This opens the Participants window on the right
  5. At the bottom of the Participants window, click Claim host button
  6. Type in your six digit Host Key (from step 23)
  7. Click the blue Claim button
  8. You're now in as host!

Step 7 About Recording Zoom Meetings

Recording Policy:
  • As a general matter, your students will appreciate receiving a recording of your class
  • Classes in which personal information is being shared may be an exception
  • Please use your discretion and share your policy with your class members
  • BOLLI members agree to be recorded when they register
  • If you do record, please inform your class members as a courtesy

Stopping Recording After Class

At the end of the class session, if you did not record automatically:

  1. Hit stop recording at the bottom center of your screen
  2. This stops the recording for the rest of the time that Zoom is on
  3. Helpful if you have conversations with members after class that you don't need in the recording

Accessing Your Zoom Recordings

Cloud Storage: Your Zoom recordings will be accessible in the cloud for 180 days

Email Notification:

  • You should receive an email from Zoom several hours after the session
  • Subject line: "Cloud Recording - <Class Name> is now available"
  • Emails go to your @brandeis.edu email address
Accessing Brandeis Email:
  • Go to go.brandeis.edu/gmail
  • Enter your Brandeis username and password
üí° Better Method: Avoid accessing your Brandeis email by going directly to the Recordings tab in your Brandeis Zoom Account
