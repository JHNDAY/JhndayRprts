BOLLI Strategic Planning Focus Groups

Comprehensive Analysis of Major Interests and Concerns - All Four Groups

Focus Group 1

Veteran Leaders

Major Interests
  • Academic Rigor
    Value challenging courses and high-level intellectual discussion
  • Community Building
    Emphasize social aspects, friendships, and crossroads interactions
  • Member-Taught Courses
    Prefer peer learning over outside experts
  • In-Person Engagement
    See physical presence as essential for community
Major Concerns
  • Loss of Community
    Due to increased Zoom usage
  • Over-Professionalization
    Worry about losing peer-learning model
  • Physical Plant Issues
    Basement location unhealthy, unsafe, uninspiring
  • Technology Support
    Inadequate for teaching and learning
  • Declining Participation
    In teaching and volunteering

Focus Group 2

Mixed Experience Members

Major Interests
  • Flexibility & Accessibility
    Appreciate Zoom options for various life circumstances
  • Academic Excellence
    Value high-quality instruction regardless of format
  • Social Connections
    Want community but understand constraints
  • Program Variety
    Different course lengths, formats, and topics
Major Concerns
  • Community Building Challenges
    Difficulty making personal connections, especially on Zoom
  • Cost and Value
    Pricing structure may not fit all needs
  • Physical Accessibility
    Transportation, mobility, and safety issues
  • Information Gaps
    Don't always know about available opportunities
  • Teacher Quality Variation
    Inconsistent experiences with different instructors

Focus Group 3

Diverse Tenure Members

Major Interests
  • Inclusivity & Accessibility
    Ensuring BOLLI serves members with different needs
  • Institutional Connection
    Stronger ties to Brandeis University
  • Innovation & Adaptation
    Learning from other programs and evolving
  • Community Outreach
    Attracting diverse membership including distant participants
Major Concerns
  • Marketing & Visibility
    Terrible website, lack of promotion, poor first impressions
  • Member Recruitment
    Especially getting more peer teachers
  • Technology Support
    Need better help for less tech-savvy members
  • Communication Failures
    Missing links, unclear processes, information gaps
  • Competitive Landscape
    Expensive compared to alternatives, need to demonstrate value

Focus Group 4

"Newish" Members (Post-COVID Joiners)

Major Interests
  • Welcoming Atmosphere
    Very welcoming environment for new members
  • In-Person Classes & Excursions
    Strong preference for in-person activities
  • Curriculum Richness
    Better program than alternatives (Tufts, UMass Boston)
  • Excellent SGL Support
    Strong support system for new teachers
Major Concerns
  • Lack of Diversity
    Not enough diversity in membership
  • Curriculum Too Jewish
    2 people concerned; others appreciated this
  • Not Enough In-Person
    "Learning is a social experience"
  • Over-Reliance on Volunteers
    "When I have an idea, there's nobody to implement it"
  • Zoom Continues COVID Isolation
    Epidemic of loneliness continues
  • Catalog Too Complicated
    Difficult to navigate and understand
Unique Program Ideas
  • Cross-OLLI Collaborations
    Joint programs with other OLLIs
  • Mini-Courses & One-Offs
    Shorter format options on special topics
  • New Member Orientation
    Help understand lottery, catalog, processes
  • Regional Sites
    North and south shore locations
  • Applied Technology Series
    GPS, AI, smartphones, drones, etc.

Cross-Cutting Themes Across All Four Groups

Shared Interests

  • High-quality learning experiences
  • Sense of community and belonging
  • Intellectual stimulation and growth
  • Peer learning opportunities

Shared Concerns

  • Balancing Zoom vs. in-person programming
  • Maintaining community connections
  • Ensuring financial accessibility
  • Improving communication and information sharing
  • Physical plant/facility limitations
