Key Themes

Most Critical Insight

BOLLI faces a fundamental tension between its core mission of creating an intellectual learning community versus practical realities of an aging population, technology adoption, economic pressures, and post-COVID behavioral changes. Success will require intentional strategies to preserve community while adapting to these realities rather than simply responding reactively to external pressures. BOLLI is at a crossroads where it must make deliberate choices about its future identity and operations.

Core Strengths

Community & Relationships
Social connections consistently cited as greatest value; Special Interest Groups (especially New Yorker group) serve as community anchors; lasting friendships central to experience
Academic Quality
High intellectual rigor praised; variety of course offerings seen as major strength; both member-taught and external courses valued

Critical Challenges

Physical Facility Crisis
Turner Street basement widely criticized for poor ventilation, accessibility, safety concerns; described as unhealthy and unwelcoming
Community Connection Breakdown
New members struggle to integrate; Zoom hinders relationship-building; multiple stories of isolation
Technology & Communication Gaps
Inadequate tech support; outdated website; communication failures; missing basic tools like name tags

The Zoom vs. In-Person Tension

Pro-Zoom:
  • Essential for accessibility
  • Supports caregivers
  • Enables distant participation
  • Accommodates mobility issues
Pro-In-Person:
  • Critical for community building
  • Better discussions
  • Full BOLLI experience
  • Authentic relationships
Root Cause
Instructor preference/convenience rather than member demand driving the shift to Zoom

Systemic Issues

Instructor Pipeline Problems
Shift from member-led to external instructors has budget implications; member-taught courses often most popular
Course Registration Dysfunction
Lottery system causes frustration; popular courses fill while others cancelled; poor course marketing
Cost & Value Misalignment
Expensive compared to alternatives; financial barriers; limited pricing flexibility

Demographic Challenges

Aging Membership Impact
Physical limitations affect participation; transportation challenges; need accessibility balance
Economic Diversity Barriers
Cost driving members to alternatives; financial aid recipients show profound gratitude

Strategic Recommendations

Prioritize Facility Improvement
Affects health, safety, and first impressions
Systematic Community Integration
Develop processes for new member integration
Technology Infrastructure Investment
Support systems and website redesign
Member Instructor Incentives
Create support and encouragement systems
Flexible Pricing Models
Improve accessibility and retention
