Barriers Encountered by Students

🔥
Firewall Blocking Brandeis Connection
A student's son had configured a personal firewall that rejected inbound Brandeis connections. This prevented her from accessing the course materials at the start of class.
Affected: 1 in-room student
🖥️
Remote Students Could Not Interact with Instructor's Screen
Fred Kaplan and others on Zoom could only watch the shared screen — they could not click, scroll, or interact with it. This created a fundamental asymmetry between in-room and remote participants during hands-on exercises.
Affected: All remote participants
🌐
Safari/Chrome Incompatibility
The token-prediction visualization did not run reliably in Safari, the dominant browser among BOLLI students. The instructor had to switch to Chrome and a separate virtual desktop to demonstrate it, adding complexity to the workflow.
Affected: Safari users (~50% of class)
📋
Copy-Paste Failures on Mac
At least one student was able to copy text from Lincoln's address but could not paste it into Claude. The context (browser security settings or focus issues) was unclear; in-room assistance was needed.
Affected: 1+ in-room students
🎙️
Voice Mode Failure (Audio Hardware)
When John demonstrated Claude's voice conversation mode with the Lincoln text, the audio did not play correctly — likely due to a hardware or sound routing issue. Students could read Claude's text response but did not hear the spoken version.
Affected: Whole class (demo failure)
📶
Ed Murrow Avatar — Choppy Network
The BOLLI/Brandeis network bandwidth caused repeated audio dropouts during the Ed Murrow avatar demonstration, leaving the avatar saying only "I'm listening…" multiple times. The conversation potential was visible but not fully realized.
Affected: Whole class (demo degraded)
⌨️
PC Users Confused by Mac Keyboard Shortcuts
One student couldn't replicate the split-screen workflow on a PC — Mac-centric instructions (F3, Command-T, green circle) didn't map directly. Some participants had old keyboards with different key layouts.
Affected: PC users in class
🔄
Conceptual Confusion: Split Screen Purpose
Marie Danziger asked the clarifying question that revealed many students didn't understand why they were setting up a split screen. The goal (tutorial on left, live browser on right for practice) wasn't self-evident from the demonstration.
Affected: Multiple remote students
🔒
Privacy Concern — Uploading Personal Documents
One student asked directly: "If I upload a personal document to Claude, can I trust it to keep my security?" This reflects a real and common anxiety about AI privacy that required an honest non-guarantee response.
Affected: Multiple students (unspoken concern)
04

Barriers Overcome

✅
Firewall: Restart & Room PC Backup
The instructor (Ollie Curme) restarted the student's computer to clear the firewall conflict. The in-room PC was also identified as a fallback option, keeping the student in class.
✅
Safari Demo: Switched to Chrome + New Desktop
John used Mac's Mission Control to spin up a clean second desktop running Chrome, demonstrating the token-prediction tool successfully while leaving the main Safari session intact.
✅
Split Screen: Clarified Purpose, Repeated Walkthrough
After Marie Danziger's clarifying question, the instructor gave a clean conceptual summary: tutorial on left, empty browser on right, switch between them to learn-by-doing. The class voted to restart the exercise from scratch rather than press on.
✅
Post-Class One-on-One Offered
"There's no class after this one. I'll stay and we'll do this slowly at your desk." This helped students who couldn't keep up in real-time maintain dignity and get the practice they needed.
✅
Marie Danziger's Email-to-AI Workaround
A student shared that she emails text directly to Claude or ChatGPT rather than downloading and re-uploading files — a peer-contributed workflow discovery that the instructor validated on the spot.
✅
"Move Tab to New Window" — Right-Click Shortcut
When students struggled to drag tabs to create split windows, John introduced the right-click "Move Tab to New Window" option as a reliable fallback that doesn't require precise mouse dragging.
✅
Remote Limitation Acknowledged & Reframed
"This is a limitation of trying to do this remotely. In-room participants have the advantage they can look up and be in their own environment." Naming the asymmetry reduced frustration and set realistic expectations.
✅
Ed Murrow: Demonstrated the Potential Despite Glitches
Despite bandwidth issues, a clean exchange about the Murrow Broadway play came through. Student reaction — "I thought his response about morality was pretty good. You can see what the potential is." — validated the concept even in degraded form.
