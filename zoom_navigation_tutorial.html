Navigating between Zoom and your work

Two approaches for moving smoothly between the instructor's shared screen and your own browser — choose the one that suits you best.

Approach 1 — Alt/Cmd + Tab

Best when you have just Zoom and one browser window open

No setup needed Works on all laptops One keystroke
Golden rule: Keep only two things open — Zoom and your browser. One tab in the browser is ideal. Do not open a second browser window.

Windows PC

1
Hold Alt and tap Tab once
What you see
A dark overlay appears in the center of the screen showing a row of application icons — one for Zoom, one for your browser. The currently selected icon has a blue highlight box around it.

Minimizing Zoom does not disconnect you from the meeting. Your audio stays live and the shared screen is waiting — you are simply moving Zoom out of your way temporarily.

Approach 2 — Virtual desktops

Best when you want multiple browser windows open alongside Zoom

Two complete environments One keystroke to switch Small setup required
The idea: Think of two desks. Desktop 1 has Zoom on it. Desktop 2 has your browser work arranged however you like. One keystroke moves you from desk to desk.

Setup — do this once at the start of class

Windows

1
Press Win + Tab
What you see
The screen zooms out to show Task View — thumbnails of all open windows and a row of desktops along the top. You will see "Desktop 1" and a "+ New desktop" button beside it.

Switching — once set up, this is all you need

Windows

1
Press Win + Ctrl + → to go to Desktop 2
2
Press Win + Ctrl + ← to return to Desktop 1
What you see while switching
The entire screen slides sideways like turning a page — your Zoom environment slides off to the left and your browser environment slides in from the right, or vice versa.

Mac

1
Press Ctrl + → to go to Space 2
2
Press Ctrl + ← to return to Space 1
3
Or swipe left/right with three fingers on the trackpad
What you see while switching
The screen slides smoothly sideways — your Zoom environment slides away and your browser workspace slides into view. The trackpad swipe makes this feel especially natural on a Mac.
This approach takes about two minutes to set up at the start of a session. Once in place it is the most powerful option — your Zoom environment and work environment are completely independent, and switching between them is always one keystroke or swipe away.
Practice drill: At the start of class, spend 60 seconds switching back and forth three times using whichever method you chose. Doing it once before you need it means you will never fumble during an exercise.
