Two Ways AI Engages With Course Material

Understanding the difference between AI that recalls from training and AI that reads and uses curated sources

BOLLI Study Group Leaders · AI Literacy Series

✍️ Basic Generative AI: The Foundation

▼

Generates text word-by-word by predicting what's most likely to come next—drawing entirely on patterns absorbed during training.

▶️ See How it Works

When you ask a basic generative AI about a topic in your BOLLI course, it answers from memory—the compressed residue of billions of web pages, books, and articles absorbed before its training cutoff. It cannot read your syllabus, your assigned readings, or your session notes. It does not know what your group discussed last week, or what your specific course materials say. It is, in effect, a very well-read scholar who has never seen your particular curriculum.

Example — What basic Gen AI can do well

"Explain the major themes in Rosalind Franklin's contributions to X-ray crystallography" — this is stable historical and scientific knowledge the model absorbed during training and can synthesize fluently.

Example — Where basic Gen AI falls short

"What did our assigned reading say about Franklin's relationship with Wilkins?" — the model has no access to your specific course text. It may confabulate a plausible-sounding but fabricated answer drawn from generic training data, not your actual materials.

⚡ RAG: The Cosmic Leap Forward

▼

Uses the same word-by-word generation process, but first retrieves your actual documents—syllabi, assigned readings, session transcripts, course notes—and grounds every answer in those specific sources, with citations.

RAG-enabled AI tools (such as Claude or ChatGPT with document upload, or Perplexity AI) do not rely solely on training memory. They query your uploaded course materials in real time, pull the most relevant passages, and then generate a response anchored to actual documents you can verify. The AI is no longer recalling generic knowledge—it is reading and synthesizing your specific materials.

Example — RAG in action for course preparation

"Based on this week's assigned reading on Rosalind Franklin, what three discussion questions would probe the most contested interpretations?" — a RAG system reads the actual uploaded text and crafts questions grounded in its specific arguments and evidence, with page references.

Example — RAG applied to session follow-up

Upload your Zoom session transcript and ask: "Where did our discussion diverge most sharply from the assigned reading's conclusions, and what additional sources might resolve those gaps?" RAG reads both documents and produces a sourced synthesis in seconds.

💡 The Foundation and the Leap

▼

Generative AI is the foundational technology—the remarkable ability to predict and generate fluent, coherent text. This is what makes AI feel intelligent. But for BOLLI course work, it is inherently generic: it knows the world, not your course.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) builds on this foundation but represents a cosmic step forward for course-specific work. It transforms AI from a well-trained but generic scholar into a live research partner that can read your actual syllabus, cross-reference your assigned texts, and cite every claim it makes back to your specific course materials.

Think of it this way: Generative AI is a brilliant colleague who has read everything—but nothing you've assigned. RAG is that same colleague—but now with your entire course library spread open on the desk.

🎓 Why RAG is a Cosmic Leap for BOLLI Course Work

▼

RAG doesn't just improve AI—it fundamentally changes what kind of question you can ask. Basic generative AI is a remarkable tool for background context, concept explanation, and general synthesis. But BOLLI course work lives in the specific: the question isn't "What do scholars generally say about Rosalind Franklin?"—it's "What does this week's assigned reading argue about Franklin, and where does our group's discussion push back?"

The transformation is concrete: With RAG-enabled AI, a Study Group Leader can prepare by asking: "Based on this syllabus and these session transcripts, identify the three recurring themes our group keeps returning to—and suggest readings that would deepen them." The AI reads your actual documents—not generic summaries of documents—and produces a sourced analysis grounded in materials your group has actually engaged with. Every claim is traceable.

For a BOLLI group spanning diverse intellectual backgrounds and disciplines, this grounding in your specific materials is not a technical nicety—it is the foundation of genuinely rigorous lifelong learning. RAG allows the SGL to show participants: here is what our assigned reading actually said, here is the passage, here is where our discussion diverged from it.

  • Document-Grounded Discussion Questions: Upload your assigned reading and ask for questions that probe its most contested claims—the AI reads the actual text, not a generic summary
  • Cross-Course Synthesis: Ask "How does the argument in Week 3's biology reading connect to the historical framing from Week 1?" and get an analysis grounded in both documents
  • Session Transcript Analysis: Upload Zoom session transcripts and ask the AI to identify recurring themes, unresolved questions, or conceptual gaps across multiple sessions—with citations back to specific moments in the discussion
  • Syllabus Gap Analysis: Compare multiple syllabi and ask "What important perspectives or voices are missing from this sequence of readings?"—grounded in what's actually there
  • Reducing Hallucination Where It Matters Most: Course-specific claims are exactly where basic Gen AI hallucination risk is highest—RAG anchors responses to your real documents, dramatically lowering that risk
  • Portal-Ready Output: Retrieve, synthesize, and format session summaries and AfterWord documents ready to share with your group, with citations linking back to the source readings

The Bottom Line: Generative AI gave us an AI that can think and write. RAG gives us an AI that can read your syllabus, analyze your transcripts, and cite every claim back to your actual course materials—transforming BOLLI course preparation from educated guesswork into systematic, evidence-based pedagogy grounded in what your group has actually read and discussed. That is the cosmic leap that makes RAG an indispensable partner for serious BOLLI course work.
