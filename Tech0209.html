Key Insights & Next Actions

üí° Breakthrough Insights

The "Just-in-Time" Learning Paradox

"It's not important to you until you need it" - Jerry's observation captured the fundamental challenge: comprehensive tutorials are overwhelming, but people can't search for solutions to problems they don't know exist.

AI as Tutorial Navigator

Instead of expecting people to read through extensive documentation, use AI to interrogate the source material on demand - turning a static tutorial into an intelligent, conversational learning assistant.

Teaching Meta-Skills

John Rudy's revelation: Instead of teaching 50 specific tricks, teach the meta-skill of using AI to find answers. "They can go to Gemini or Claude and ask a question" becomes more valuable than remembering specific procedures.

Screen Capture as Bridge

Copying a screen image and asking Claude "how do I do this?" creates a natural bridge between visual confusion and textual solution - especially powerful for people who struggle with technical vocabulary.

Vocabulary as Barrier

Technical jargon creates discovery problems. As John Rudy noted about car manuals: "they use words for things that are different than the word that I would use for it." AI helps bridge this gap.
