September-October 2025
Visualizing Foundational Computer Science
Created 12 comprehensive data visualizations of Alan Turing's "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" (1950). Explored Turing's methodological revolution from philosophical questions ("What is thinking?") to operational tests (imitation game). Used both Claude and ChatGPT to generate and catalogue visualizations.
Pivotal Insight: Discovered that Turing's genius was not answering "Can machines think?" but reframing the question itself. His move from metaphysical inquiry to empirical testing created the entire field of AI. Visualization revealed how foundational papers contain methodological breakthroughs as important as their specific conclusions. Demonstrated AI's power in making complex historical texts accessible through multiple visual representations.
