Finding Your Keys

EASY
Learn to identify and locate keyboard modifier keys on Mac and Windows

Understanding Keyboard Modifiers

Before you can use keyboard shortcuts, you need to know where the special modifier keys are located. These keys are different on Mac and Windows!

üçé Mac Keyboard

  • ‚åò Command - Located next to the spacebar (both sides)
  • ‚å• Option (Alt) - Located next to Command keys
  • ‚áß Shift - Large keys at far left and right
  • ‚åÉ Control - Far left, below Shift

ü™ü Windows Keyboard

  • Ctrl (Control) - Bottom left and right corners
  • Alt - Next to the spacebar (both sides)
  • Shift - Large keys at far left and right
  • ‚äû Windows - Between Ctrl and Alt (left side)
üí° Key Translation: When you see Mac shortcuts, translate them to Windows:
  • ‚åò Command (Mac) = Ctrl (Windows)
  • ‚å• Option (Mac) = Alt (Windows)
  • Shift is the same on both!

Practice: Find These Keys

1 Look at your keyboard right now
üçé Mac Users: Find the Command key (‚åò). It's the key right next to your spacebar. There's one on each side!
ü™ü Windows Users: Find the Ctrl key. It's in the bottom left corner of your keyboard. There's also one on the right side!
2 Place your fingers on the modifier keys - Get comfortable with their locations
3 Practice pressing them - Try pressing each modifier key a few times
Checkpoint: Can you do this?
  • I can find the main modifier key on my keyboard (‚åò on Mac / Ctrl on Windows)
  • I can find the Option/Alt key quickly
  • I know where both Shift keys are located
  • I understand the Mac-to-Windows key translation

‚ö†Ô∏è Platform Differences: Screenshot methods are VERY different between Mac and Windows. Follow your platform's instructions carefully!

üçé Mac Screenshot Shortcuts

‚Üí Captures entire screen to clipboard
‚Üí Paste into a document or image editor

‚Üí Captures only the currently active window

Split Screen & Window Management

MEDIUM
View multiple windows side-by-side for efficient multitasking

Why Split Screen Matters

During BOLLI Zoom sessions, you often need to see multiple things at once: the Zoom window, course materials, and your notes. Split screen makes this easy!

‚ö†Ô∏è Platform Differences: Mac and Windows have completely different split screen methods. Follow your platform's instructions!

üçé Mac Split Screen

1 Find the green button - It's in the top-left corner of any window (red, yellow, green dots)
2 Click and HOLD the green button - Don't just click! Hold it down for a moment
3 Choose a side - You'll see options to tile the window left or right. Choose one!
4 Select the second window - Your other open windows will appear. Click one to put it on the other side
üí° Quick Method: Hold ‚å• Option and click the green button to instantly tile the window to one side!
5 Adjust the divider - Drag the vertical line between windows to adjust sizes
6 Exit split screen:
  • Press Esc, or
  • Move cursor to top and click the green button again, or
  • Click another desktop in Mission Control

ü™ü Windows Snap (Split Screen)

1 Keyboard Method (Fastest):
  • Make sure a window is active (click on it)
  • Press ‚äû Win + ‚Üê (left arrow) to snap window to left half
  • Press ‚äû Win + ‚Üí (right arrow) to snap window to right half
2 Mouse Method:
  • Click and drag a window's title bar to the left or right edge of your screen
  • When the outline appears, release to snap it into place
3 Select the second window - Windows will show your other open windows. Click one to fill the other half
4 Adjust the divider - Drag the vertical line between windows to adjust sizes
üí° Quarter Screen Snap: Use ‚äû Win + Arrow keys in combinations:
  • Win + Up = Top half
  • Win + Down = Bottom half
  • Win + Left then Up = Top-left quarter
  • Win + Right then Down = Bottom-right quarter

BOLLI Zoom Setup Practice

1 Open two windows:
  • Left: Open Chrome to a BOLLI course page or Zoom
  • Right: Open a Notes app or Google Doc
2 Use split screen - Follow your platform's method to put Chrome on the left and Notes on the right
3 Practice working in both - Try taking notes while reading the course page
üéØ Perfect BOLLI Setup:
  • Left: Zoom meeting window (see instructor and participants)
  • Right: Course materials or note-taking app
  • Now you can participate in class while referencing materials and taking notes!
Checkpoint: Split Screen Mastery
  • I know how to activate split screen on my platform
  • I can create a split screen in under 10 seconds
  • I can adjust the split to my preferred layout
  • I know how to exit split screen
  • I understand how to set up Zoom + materials for class

These shortcuts work in Chrome, Word, email, and almost every application! They're fundamental computer skills.

üçé Mac Text Shortcuts
