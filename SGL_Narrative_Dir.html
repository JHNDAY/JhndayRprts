Grit & Grace: Short Stories of Larry Brown & Tim Gautreaux
by Marlene Hobel
A literary course exploring the short fiction of two contemporary Southern writers: Larry Brown (Mississippi) and Tim Gautreaux (Louisiana). Brown writes in the "dirty realism" tradition with raw, unflinching stories, while Gautreaux employs comic realism with Cajun characters experiencing moments of grace. Both capture working-class Southern life with distinct literary styles.

My Good Bright Wolf: Understanding Anorexia Through the Lens of Memoir
by Judy Feldman
A course using Sarah Moss's memoir "My Good Bright Wolf" to understand the psychological, medical, and sociological aspects of anorexia. Topics include early signs, family dynamics, triggers throughout the life cycle, treatment spectrum, recovery, and the value of memoir as therapy and education for patients and families dealing with eating disorders.

by Kate (Ehrlich) Seideman
A course examining historical examples of injustice toward women through five fiction films from the US, England, and Australia. by Henry Morris
A course exploring the development of contemporary radical Jewish philosophies (Reconstructionism, neo-Hasidism/Renewal, Feminism/LGBTQ) and their roots in Spinoza's 17th century critique of traditional Judaism. by John Day
A 5-week course on the history of the Iberian Peninsula from the first Islamic incursion in 711 to the Reconquista in 1252.
