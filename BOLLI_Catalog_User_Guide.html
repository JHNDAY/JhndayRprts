3. Duration Filter

The Duration Filter helps you find courses based on their length and start date. This is particularly useful if you have scheduling constraints or want to take courses of specific durations.

Duration Options

‚è≥ 10 Weeks (March Start)

Full-semester courses starting in early March and running through May. These courses provide in-depth exploration of subjects.

üìÖ 5 Weeks (March Start)

First half-semester courses starting in March. Perfect for focused study or if you want to sample different subjects.

üìÜ 5 Weeks (April Start)

Second half-semester courses starting in April. Great for adding courses mid-semester or lighter spring commitments.

How to Use the Duration Filter

  1. Locate the Duration dropdown
    Find it in the filter bar at the top of the catalog, alongside Modality and Category filters.
  2. Select your preferred duration
    Choose from "10 weeks March," "5 weeks March," "5 weeks April," or "All Durations."
  3. View filtered results
    The course list and calendar both update instantly to show only courses matching your duration selection.
  4. Combine with other filters
    Use Duration alongside Modality, Category, and Search for precise course discovery.

üí° Planning Strategy with Duration Filter

Example Workflow:

  1. Start by filtering "10 weeks March" to see full-semester commitments
  2. Add a Category filter (e.g., "History") to narrow by subject
  3. Check the calendar to see when these courses meet
  4. Then switch to "5 weeks April" to find lighter courses that fit around your longer commitments

Result: A balanced schedule with depth and variety!

Duration and Commitment Planning

Understanding course duration helps you:

  • Balance your schedule: Mix 10-week deep dives with 5-week samplers
  • Manage preparation time: Shorter courses often have different pacing
  • Plan around travel: Choose 5-week courses if you'll be away mid-semester
  • Try new subjects: 5-week courses are perfect for exploring new interests

4. My Courses Feature

The "My Courses" feature lets you build and save a personalized schedule as you explore the catalog. Think of it as your shopping cart for course selection!

Adding Courses to My Courses

  1. Browse the catalog
    Use filters and search to find courses that interest you.
  2. Click course cards to expand
    Read the full description, leadership style, materials, and preparation time.
  3. Click "üìö Add to My Courses"
    Each course card has an "Add to My Courses" button. Click it to save the course to your personal list.
  4. Receive confirmation
    You'll see an alert confirming the course was added and showing your total course count.

Viewing Your Course Schedule

  1. Click the "üìö My Courses" button
    Located in the top navigation bar. A badge shows how many courses you've saved.
  2. Review your selections
    See all your saved courses with complete details: title, leader, time, day, duration, modality, and description.

Organizing Your Schedule with Drag and Drop

One of the most powerful features of My Courses is the ability to reorder your courses by simply dragging and dropping them. This lets you arrange your schedule exactly how you want to see it!

How to Reorder Courses

  1. Locate the drag handle
    Each course card has a vertical dots icon (‚ãÆ‚ãÆ) on the left side‚Äîthis is your drag handle.
  2. Click and hold
    Click and hold on the drag handle. The course card will become slightly transparent and your cursor will change to a "grabbing" hand.
  3. Drag to new position
    While holding, drag the course card up or down to your desired position. Other cards will show a dashed blue border when you hover over them, indicating where the course will be placed.
  4. Release to drop
    Release the mouse button to drop the course in its new position. The order saves automatically!

Visual Guide: Drag and Drop in Action

Before dragging:

  • Course cards appear in the order they were added
  • Drag handle (‚ãÆ‚ãÆ) visible on the left of each course
  • Cursor shows normal pointer

While dragging:

  • Selected card becomes semi-transparent (50% opacity)
  • Cursor changes to "grabbing" hand
  • Target position shows dashed blue border and light blue background

After dropping:

  • Course appears in new position
  • New order saves automatically to your browser
  • All courses return to normal appearance

Smart Ways to Use Drag and Drop

üìÖ Organize by Day

Group all Monday courses together, then Tuesday courses, etc. This creates a chronological weekly view of your schedule.

‚è∞ Sort by Time

Arrange courses in daily time order (Period 1 ‚Üí Period 2 ‚Üí Period 3) to visualize your daily schedule flow.

üéØ Prioritize Favorites

5. Interactive Calendar Grid

The dynamic calendar shows course distribution across the week, helping you visualize when courses are offered and identify scheduling patterns.

How the Calendar Works

The calendar displays a grid with:

  • Rows: Three time periods (Period 1: 9:30-10:55am, Period 2: 11:10am-12:35pm, Period 3: 2:10-3:35pm)
  • Columns: Days of the week (Monday through Friday)
  • Cell Content: Number of courses offered at each day/time combination

Using the Calendar

  1. Observe the distribution
    See at a glance which days/times have the most course options.
  2. Apply filters
    Watch the calendar update in real-time as you filter by modality, category, duration, or search terms.
  3. Click a cell
    Click any cell with courses to jump directly to those specific offerings.
  4. Selected cells highlight
    The selected time slot highlights in purple, making it easy to see which courses you're viewing.
  5. Clear selection
    Use the "Clear Selection" button to return to viewing all courses.

Reading the Calendar

  • Large numbers: More course options at that time
  • Gray cells: No courses match current filters for that time slot
  • Purple highlight: Currently selected time slot
  • Hover effect: Cells scale slightly on hover for better interactivity

üí° Pro Tip

Use the dynamic calendar to identify your "sweet spots" - days and times when multiple interesting courses are offered. This helps maximize your options while planning around other commitments.
