  1. Click any cell with courses

  2. Review the filtered results

  3. Clear the selection
    Click the "Clear Selection" button above the calendar to see all courses again.
  4. Combine with other filters
    After selecting a time slot, use modality or category filters to narrow further. The calendar will maintain your time selection while showing updated counts.

4. Browsing Courses

Once you've applied your desired filters (or viewing all courses), you'll see courses organized by day and period.

Course Organization

Courses are grouped in a hierarchical structure:

  1. Day Headers
    Each day (Monday-Friday) has a collapsible header showing the total course count.
  2. Period Sections
    Within each day, courses are grouped by Period 1, 2, or 3 with time ranges shown.
  3. Course Cards
    Individual courses appear as cards within each period section.

  2. Read the information

  3. Click again to collapse
    The card returns to its compact view.

6. Leader Biographies

Learn about the expertise and background of course leaders through comprehensive biographies.

Accessing Biographies

  1. From a course card
    Click the "View Bio ‚Üí" link next to any leader's name. The page will scroll smoothly to their biography.
  2. From the biography section
    Scroll to the "Course Leader Biographies" section at the bottom of the page to browse all leaders alphabetically.
  3. Search biographies
    Use the search box in the biography section to find leaders by name or keyword.

Biography Features

  • Alphabetical Organization: All leaders listed A-Z for easy browsing
  • Searchable: Find leaders by name or search their biography text
  • Highlighted Navigation: When you click a "View Bio" link, the biography briefly highlights in yellow
  • Complete Background: Education, experience, expertise, and teaching philosophy

üí° Pro Tip

If you're interested in courses by a particular leader, search for their name in the main search box at the top. This will show all courses they're teaching.

Planning Your Schedule with Dynamic Filtering

  1. Start with your constraints
    Filter by modality if you prefer online or in-person. The calendar updates to show only those course distributions.
  2. Explore subject interests
    Filter by category (e.g., "Literature," "History") and watch the calendar reveal when those courses cluster. This helps you spot opportunities.
  3. Identify patterns in the dynamic calendar
    Notice which days/periods have the most options for your interests. Use this visual feedback to plan your ideal schedule.
  4. Click promising time slots
    Select cells in the calendar that fit your schedule and have good course counts. Review the specific courses offered.
  5. Read full descriptions
    Expand courses to understand content depth and requirements. Check preparation time and materials needed.
  6. Balance your load
    Note preparation times and mix in-person/Zoom courses for optimal flexibility and manageable weekly commitments.
