04 · OVERALL VERDICT

Which Is Right for You?

Best for
Vanguard
📌 Long-term, buy-and-hold investors

Vanguard's philosophy is discipline over dynamism. If you want to set a course and stay on it — retirement-focused, diversification-first — Vanguard's toolset is purpose-built for you. Less exciting, more enduring.

KEY FRAMING

At 75 with $2M and no debt, the investment mandate has shifted fundamentally — away from accumulation and toward capital preservation, reliable income generation, and estate & legacy planning. Active trading, crypto, and high-growth tools are largely irrelevant here. That reframing changes the comparison considerably.

VANGUARD · ASSESSMENT
Stronger Philosophical Fit
The ethos matches the moment
✔ Ideal funds for this profile
VWINX (Wellesley Income) and VWELX (Wellington) were essentially designed for conservative, income-focused retirees. Balanced, disciplined, and time-tested.
✔ Lowest costs in absolute dollars
At $2M, even tiny expense ratio differences matter. Vanguard's minimalist fee structure is purpose-built for large, long-held portfolios.
✔ Personal Advisor Services ($1M+)
Hybrid human + robo advisory at ~0.3% — reasonable for assets of this scale.
⚠ Weakness
Tools and customer service historically more basic. Less robust for complex estate planning support.
FIDELITY · ASSESSMENT
Stronger on Service
Richer wealth management infrastructure
✔ Fidelity Wealth Services ($250K+)
Dedicated advisor access, estate planning support, and a superior client service experience at this asset level.
✔ Better RMD tools
Both spouses are past RMD age (73 under current law). Fidelity's RMD calculators and tax management tools are meaningfully more robust.
✔ Full-service wealth platform
Better infrastructure for managing complex needs: estate coordination, beneficiary planning, and tax-efficient withdrawal strategies.
⚠ Note
Trading platform bells and whistles (crypto, options) are irrelevant here — don't let Fidelity's active-trader branding obscure its strong wealth management tier.
~$300
Per $1M · Per Year

The expense ratio difference is noise at this scale. The gap between 0.015% (Fidelity FXAIX) and 0.03% (Vanguard VOO) amounts to roughly $300/year per $1M invested — a rounding error compared to the impact of asset allocation decisions, tax strategy, and withdrawal sequencing.

DECISION GUIDE
CHOOSE VANGUARD IF…
  • → Priority is simplicity and lowest possible costs
  • → A set-it-and-don't-touch-it philosophy resonates
  • → Income-focused funds like Wellesley (VWINX) are the target vehicle
  • → Estate plan is already handled externally by an attorney
CHOOSE FIDELITY IF…
  • → Richer advisor access and estate planning support is needed
  • → Better RMD management and tax-efficiency tools are a priority
  • → Full-service wealth management experience is preferred
  • → Consolidated account management with dedicated advisor contact matters
⚑ STRONGEST RECOMMENDATION

At $2M and age 75, neither platform's self-directed or robo tools are the right primary solution. This is a situation where a fee-only fiduciary financial planner — independent of both firms — should lead on asset allocation, RMD sequencing, Social Security optimization, and estate planning.

Vanguard or Fidelity then serves as the custodian of whatever strategy that advisor recommends — not the advisor itself. The choice of platform matters far less than the quality of the plan it holds.
