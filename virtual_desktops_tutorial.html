Working with virtual desktops

Virtual desktops let you maintain two complete, independent work environments and switch between them instantly with a single keystroke — keeping your Zoom session and your work neatly separated at all times.

Why this approach works well

Each desktop holds its full window arrangement — nothing moves or collapses
Switching is one keystroke — no hunting, no clicking
Zoom stays on its own desktop and can never get buried under other windows
Your work desktop can have as many browser windows open as you need

Step 1 — Create two desktops

Do this once at the start of your first session

Windows — Task View

1

What you see
The screen zooms out showing Task View — thumbnails of all open windows fill the screen, and a row of desktops appears along the top edge. You will see Desktop 1 and a "+ New desktop" button beside it.

Note — Mac cannot rename Spaces
Unlike Windows, macOS does not support renaming desktops. The best workaround: set a different wallpaper color on each Space so you always know at a glance which environment you are in.
Space 1
Blue = Zoom
Space 2
Green = Work
To set a wallpaper per Space: switch to that Space, right-click the desktop, choose Change Wallpaper, and pick a solid color. Each Space remembers its own. The color change as you switch is an instant visual confirmation that you have arrived in the right environment.

Step 2 — Switch between desktops

This is all you need during class — one keystroke in either direction

Windows

1
Press Win + Ctrl + → to move to the next desktop
2
Press Win + Ctrl + ← to move back
What you see while switching
The entire screen slides sideways like turning a page. Your Zoom environment slides off one side, your work environment slides in from the other. Everything is exactly where you left it.

Switching desktops does not disconnect you from Zoom. Your audio stays live and the shared screen keeps running — you have simply moved to a different desk. Switch back any time and Zoom is exactly as you left it.

Step 3 — Managing your work desktop

Arrange your browser windows on Desktop 2 however you like — they stay put when you switch away

Windows

1
Switch to Desktop 2 with Win+Ctrl+→
2
Open as many browser windows as you need and arrange them freely
3
To move a window between desktops: open Task View (Win+Tab), right-click the window thumbnail, choose Move to →

Mac

1
Switch to Space 2 with Ctrl+→
2
Open as many browser windows as you need and arrange them freely
3
To move a window to another Space: enter Mission Control (F3), then drag the window thumbnail up to the destination Space in the strip at the top
Practice drill — do this at the start of every session: Once your two desktops are set up, spend 60 seconds switching back and forth three times. Say out loud which desktop you are on each time. Doing it before the first exercise means you will never fumble mid-class.
