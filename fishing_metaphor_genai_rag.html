Hook, Line & Sinker — vs — The Cast Net

A fishing metaphor for Generative AI and RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
Generative AI — base model
One hook, one cast — one token. The model fishes a vast, invisible ocean (its training corpus) and returns a single word at a time from parametric memory. You cannot see the bottom. You cannot choose which fish are there. You can only bait the hook with your prompt and wait.
RAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation
RAG casts a net into a bounded, curated pond — a document store you built and can inspect. One cast hauls back an entire reference library: history, theory, methods, results, all at once. Only then does generation begin — reasoning over the whole haul, not fishing blindly from memory.

The first cast is yours — a broad question, sweeping the pond. You read what comes back. You notice what's missing. Then you decide to cast again with a sharper question. Each cast is driven by your reading of the last haul, not by the system. This is RAG as conversation: you ask, you read, you notice, you ask again.
