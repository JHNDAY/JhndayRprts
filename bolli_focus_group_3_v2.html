Community Building & Connection
BARBARA KRISS:
"I really have... I feel like you really can develop a sense of community through Zoom... when one of the members was leading a discussion... he said, Barbara, what about you? And I said, what? You're calling on me? And he said, well, you're a friend."
BARB CHANDLER:
"I really expected it would be a place where I could meet people who were also retired, also interested in learning... but in terms of really meeting people, getting to know people, or developing new friendships, that hasn't been my experience."

Suggestions Made:

  • Encourage volunteering on committees and SIGs as primary way to meet people
  • Structured introductions in classes (turn to person next to you, introduce each other)
  • Small group discussions rather than large gatherings
  • Online bulletin board for ride-sharing and connections
  • Continue new member meetings and outreach
Learning Preferences & Quality
ANDY URBAN:
"I have nothing but praise... for the quality and variety of classes offered. The quality of the classes, not just by the strength of the instructors. But by the strength of the participants and the variety of interests and expertise."
BARBARA KRISS:
"there's just the high level of academic interest of people, the nature of the classes themselves, just the intellectual rigor of the participants... it's exceeded my expectations."

Suggestions Made:

  • Mix of lecture and discussion works best
  • Read at home, brief SGL presentation, then discussion
  • More experiential learning opportunities
  • Both member-taught and expert-led classes valued
  • Recruit more retiring faculty and administrators
Technology & Accessibility
BONNIE ORLIN:
"when you're on Zoom, the technology really matters, and some people are more adept than others... I think some people, you know, we may need more help sometimes."
SANDRA REESE:
"For me to go to a Bali activity, is close to $20 one way on an Uber. So that's $40, which, to me, is prohibitive... I look to Bali as a place where I can co-mingle with well-educated learned people."

Suggestions Made:

  • Reform technology committee to provide device instruction
  • Acknowledge technical difficulties and provide support
  • Continue both Zoom and in-person options
  • Improve hybrid participation capabilities
  • Organized carpooling and ride-sharing
Cost vs. Value Perception
RON LEVY:
"we're the expensive one on the street. There aren't many... that is moderately more expensive than we are. And everybody else is a lot cheaper. And in some cases, almost free."
TAMARA CHERNOW:
"if you break it down, what you get at Bali. Compared to some of the others. You're getting your money's worth... with all the speakers and the activities. And the groups... there's a tremendous amount going on."

Suggestions Made:

  • Reframe marketing to emphasize value rather than cost
  • Create testimonials highlighting program richness
  • Compare favorably to expensive continuing education programs
  • Highlight all included benefits beyond just courses
  • Address cost concerns with value messaging
Website & Information Access
TAMARA CHERNOW:
"we have the worst website that I've ever seen for an organization full of smart, capable, talented people... I have to hunt and hunt and hunt if I want to tell somebody about the special interest groups... None of this is intuitive, none of this has bright colors... it's a list of words."

Suggestions Made:

  • Complete website redesign with community input
  • Make special interest groups easily discoverable
  • Use bright colors and intuitive navigation
  • Include current photos of active members
  • Create better first impression for prospective members
Programming & Offerings
SANDRA REESE:
"I wanted to keep the momentum going. But didn't see much in the way of summer courses... I wish there'd been more of an interest in offering courses."
TAMARA CHERNOW:
"more courses that are similar to the ones we have, but very short ones. In this, in this summer... so that people have something to do in the summer and the winter, that it's more continuous."

Suggestions Made:

  • Expand summer and winter course offerings
  • Offer shorter courses during break periods
  • Research other OLLI programs for innovative ideas
  • Recruit more retired professors as instructors
  • Encourage more member-taught courses

Moving Personal Testimony

SANDRA REESE:
"What I'm probably most grateful for is that I was offered a 50% scholarship this semester. Which made it possible for me to come back... I look to Bali as a place where I can co-mingle with well-educated learned people... This is a kind of a home. Because there is no other home... where I would be treated as an equal, where I would be recognized for my academic achievements."
