Religion and Politics in America

A 10-Week Course · 90 Minutes per Session

Course Overview

This course examines the relationship of religion to politics in the United States from 1600 to 2026. We will explore how religion has been a major force in American politics—from the Puritans and Pilgrims to Project 2025—serving at times as both a progressive and reactionary influence. Through chronological progression and thematic analysis, we'll understand today's church-state debates within their full historical context.

Pedagogical Notes for 90-Minute Sessions

Timing Structure

  • 5 min: Welcome, housekeeping, recap of previous week
  • 40-50 min: Core content (lecture with visuals/primary sources)
  • 30-40 min: Structured discussion
  • 5-10 min: Bridge to next week, preview reading/questions

Interactive Elements

  • Use primary source documents (projected or distributed)
  • Contemporary parallels highlighted throughout
  • Think-pair-share for complex questions
  • Anonymous question collection for sensitive topics

Scaffolding Principles

  • Each week references previous weeks' concepts
  • Chronological progression with thematic threads
  • Increasing complexity in analysis
  • Building toward contemporary understanding
